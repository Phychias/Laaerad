    Trusk Empire¶

    Wiki
    Capital [[Hasit City]]
    Official Language [[Truskean]]
    Major Ethnic Groups Truskeans, Glömezher, Laprio
    Political System Absolute Monarchy
    Core Territory Both sides of the Heimet Strait

    Trusk, as a regime name frequently mentioned in historical documents, specifically refers to the powerful state established during the late ancient period at the convergence of the three old continents of Laaerad. Its unique geographical location made it a natural hub connecting the eastern and western parts of the continent. It was not only a crucial trade route, facilitating the exchange of goods, technology, and ideas, but also a melting pot for multicultural fusion. Therefore, on the historical stage of the Laaerad world, Trusk always played a pivotal role due to its irreplaceable strategic position and profound influence.

    Geography¶

    What is Trusk?

    The territory of the Trusk Empire lies in the very center of Laaerad's eastern continent, making it a natural hub connecting various regions of the continent. It was not only a necessary passage for trade but also a center for cultural fusion. Its territory extended north to Mistnatila and south to the rugged Guduk-Athesa Mountains, which provided a solid southern defense line for the empire. The empire possessed the continent's most fertile farmland along the Novaya coast. This fertile land was the cornerstone of its prosperity, continuously providing the empire with food and wealth, supporting a large population and a powerful economy. More crucially, the Trusk Empire also firmly controlled the strategic passages between the Novaya Sea and the North Sea and South Sea—the Heimet and [[Novaya]] Straits. These key waterways gave the empire unparalleled control over maritime trade, allowing it to effectively regulate the regional economic lifeline and project its naval power. Therefore, with its exceptionally favorable geographical location and dual control over land and sea transportation arteries, the Trusk Empire became the undisputed core power on this continent.

    Topography¶

    The core region of the Trusk Empire is dominated by the vast Trusk Basin, a fertile lowland that forms the heartland of the nation. A belt extending along the Novaya Sea coast consists mostly of fertile alluvial plains. Benefiting from a Mediterranean-like climate and nourishment from rivers, the soil here is particularly suitable for cultivating wheat, barley, and various vegetables, forming the empire's most important agricultural production area.

    The western part of the empire is a region of towering, continuous mountain ranges, primarily divided into two systems: the northern Tyesuku Mountains are known for their steep peaks and rich mineral resources, especially silver and iron; while the southern Genara Mountains are famous for their dense coniferous forests and unique mountain ecosystems, serving as habitats for many rare animals. The northern border features hills, beyond which lie the grasslands of the Glömezher people. This vast grassland area is home to nomadic Glömezher tribes who live by herding reindeer and hunting. The northernmost Glömezher territory of the empire extends even further north to the Mistnatila region, considered sacred by the Glömezher. This perpetually snow-covered, cold area is not only their spiritual sanctuary but is also said to hold many unknown treasures from ancient times.

    The eastern boundary is clearly defined by the dense tropical rainforests of Anioksait. These rainforests, with their hot and humid climate, crisscrossing rivers, and many unique plant and animal species, form a natural ecological barrier. The southern terrain is predominantly mountainous. These mountains, though not as towering as those in the west, feature complex topography with interwoven valleys. Particularly noteworthy is the southwestern edge of the Qimiak region, which gradually transitions into a vast, endless golden desert. This desert experiences extreme temperature differences between day and night and frequent sandstorms, forming the empire's southernmost natural boundary.

    Climate: The Novaya with Non-Coincident Rain and Heat¶

    The core region of Trusk primarily experiences a climate with non-coincident rain and heat, but the north is also influenced by ocean currents from the North Sea, resulting in a temperate monsoon climate in parts. The southern index region is largely subtropical, with many jungles and broadleaf forests. Between the southernmost part and Long Island lie vast savannas, difficult to traverse. The southeast has extensive primeval forests and humid, hot plateaus.

    Vegetation¶

    The Mediterranean climate zone has a rich variety of plant types. The jungles and deserts of the southwest and southeast have their corresponding vegetation.

    Fauna¶

    Complex animal biomes resulting from multiple climate types. Meat livestock (poultry) mainly include chickens, ducks, sheep, and pigs; draft animals are primarily the Tapir-Luo (horse) and Ruiba (cattle).

    The Novaya Sea has abundant fishery resources. There is a great variety and quantity of fish, along with various seafood such as scallops and mussels.

    The Trusk Basin has near-river biome ecosystems along its rivers. The variety of fish within the rivers is not extensive. Meanwhile, most near-river areas have developed agriculture on the basis of the original ecology, and the area of cultivated land has continuously expanded with the advancement of farming techniques.

    Civilization¶

    Language¶

    Standard Truskean is based on the Heimet dialect. The main characteristics of the Heimet dialect are: larger mouth openings, stress often placed on the penultimate syllable, and a falling tone at the end of words.

    Truskean has numerous dialects, but they can mainly be categorized into the Laprio dialect and the Northern dialect. The Laprio dialect is widely used in southern Trusk and incorporates a large number of ancient Laprio loanwords. The Northern dialect is primarily led by Truskean speakers among the Glömezher nobility, featuring many phonetic characteristics of the Glömezher language and a more rugged auditory impression.

    For a more detailed description of dialects, please refer to the document Truskean.

    Culture¶

    Overall, Truskean culture values freedom and a spirit of adventure, but also emphasizes family and honor. This is due to Trusk's economic and political forms.

    A Nation Built on Commerce: The Merchant Culture of Trusk¶

    Trusk has had agricultural and forging production forms since ancient times. However, after entering the Heimet Dynasty period, due to Heimet's special geographical location and some legacies of the political undercurrents of the first Kuileot Dynasty, many nobles rose from merchant backgrounds. Furthermore, the shipping and trade system led by Heimet has always been one of the main economic sources for various Truskean dynasties, giving Heimet's official merchants a relatively high status within the empire. As for private merchants, their status varied by era. When the situation in Novaya and Laprio was unstable, unregistered merchants were often treated as "pirates," a status that constantly changed according to the specific needs of the ruler.

    But in summary, the official support for commerce from Trusk, the favorable climatic conditions of Novaya, the excellent shipbuilding skills of craftsmen, and the improved sealed longboats based on "magic" provided many conveniences for the commercial activities of the Trusk Empire. This also gave birth to a spirit that崇尚自由和探索 (values freedom and exploration). The folk customs were relatively relaxed, with diverse folk activities and many festivals related to navigation, which also had some connection to the Sea God信仰.

    Simultaneously, the Heimet Strait possesses many natural deep-water harbors. Ships from the Xinzhi Sea and Novaya gather here, and goods from all over the empire are distributed here. From beyond the empire, sable furs from the northern frontier, fine steeds from the Horse tribes, handicrafts from Iying, and ironware from Kuotai also arrive in succession. The various skills and arts催生 by the flourishing commerce also spurred people's search for culture and knowledge, profoundly influencing the course of Truskean civilization.

    Divine Right of Kings: Sole Supremacy on the Sea and Under Heaven¶

    Trusk's religion and aristocratic culture are two sides of the same coin. With Sea God信仰 as the core religion, combined with the aristocratic culture meticulously crafted by the Heimet family, the two互为表里 (complement each other), assimilating and controlling the thoughts of people across the empire for over a thousand years. Of course, after entering the imperial era, the mainstream ideology of the empire inevitably became rigid, shifting towards strengthening centralization of power as its core, and degenerated into an important tool for the皇室 to control the entire nation, especially the core Trusk region.

    The basis for the divine right of Truskean kings is based on this consensus: the legitimate emperor of Trusk will definitely become a Drowned God after death. That is to say, the legitimacy of the Truskean emperor stems from people's trust in his divinity, but this divinity in turn stems from the emperor's legitimacy, thus achieving a circular论证 for the legitimacy of Truskean imperial power. This theory actually lacks a solid source of legitimacy, so the dynastic changes in Trusk were not a rare occurrence. The origin of this theory is believed to be recorded in the lost scriptures of the Sea God religion, and there is much circumstantial evidence suggesting it may have formed during the first Kuileot Dynasty, but many details are now不可考据 (unverifiable).

    Consequently, despite Trusk having a relatively open atmosphere and a spirit that崇尚自由、探索 (values freedom and exploration), the hierarchical system in Trusk, from the city-state era to the imperial era, was still quite strict, let alone during the period when the Glömezher ruled Heimet. The legitimacy of Truskean nobility, in the early period, still stemmed from their control over their territories. After the Sea God religion spread throughout the nation, it gradually tended to be granted by the emperor.

    In Truskean culture, the family is also considered a very important part. Truskean society values filial piety and传承 (inheritance/legacy). For a long time, it also believed in the saying "like father, like son," which may be related to the occupational household registration system briefly implemented during the second Heimet Dynasty.

    Religion¶

    Sea God Religion¶

    The indigenous religion of Trusk is a polytheistic religion centered on the worship of Sea Gods. In the earliest city-state period, Trusk was not coastal, and the deities they供奉 (worshipped) were likely mainly mountain gods and forging gods. Entering the era of the city-state alliance, after expanding their territory to the Heimet Strait and Novaya, the Sea God信仰 gradually dominated the religious system of the entire region. In the religious context, the "sea" is not merely the physical sea itself as a body of water, but a philosophical concept, symbolizing both the chaos at the world's beginning and the flow of fate and nature like water.

    The core of the Sea God信仰 is six main gods: God Soriwar, God Gyua, God Noikpi, God Pwaso, God Taciap, God Ugwat. The first three deities are also called the Upper Three Gods, the gods who created the sea, corresponding to the origin of the world in Heimet-Trusk mythology: the world was originally一片鸿蒙 (a vast primordial chaos). The first consciousness to awaken was God Soriwar, who wove the boundless sea. God Gyua then awoke, hiding half of the sea woven by Soriwar. God Noikpi "turned over" the hidden sea, thus creating land and mountains in the world. The latter three deities are called the Lower Three Gods: God Pwaso manages shallow seas, mudflats, and beaches; God Taciap manages deep seas and weather; God Ugwat manages rivers and land. In some local records, the Lower Three Gods are considered avatars of God Noikpi, but the more mainstream view is that they are the共同子 (shared children) of the Upper Three Gods, their "shadows." These six gods may be related to the gods worshipped by the earliest city-states that joined the Trusk city-state alliance, but their prototypes are now difficult to考证 clearly. The verifiable inscriptions are only those from the Matashoksa Gate period, carved on many stone tablets along the Matashoksa Gate大道 (avenue), containing information about the initial theogony.

    After the third Heimet Dynasty, the Heimet皇室 attempted to standardize the religion, prescribing祭祀方法 (ritual methods) and the jurisdictions of each deity. God Soriwar presides over生育 (birth/fertility), God Gyua over死亡 (death), God Noikpi over命运 (fate), God Pwaso over战事 (warfare), God Taciap over行路 (travel/journeys), and God Ugwat over丰收 (harvest). A special calendar专门记载 (specifically records) the seasons for祭祀各神明 (worshipping each deity).

    Beyond the six main gods, there are also gods representing various natural elements, such as the Sun God, Moon God, Wind God, etc. These gods are all under the管辖 (jurisdiction) of their respective main gods. The Johuzai of the Laprio people have also been partially incorporated. The origins of these gods may be the deities worshipped by early city-states.

    Outside the core of Sea God信仰 lies the Drowned God信仰. The Truskeans believe that those who died at sea and returned have become gods, and their names are recorded and列入神册 (entered into the divine register). Consequently, the mainstream Truskean funeral is a sea burial. People sail to the center of the sea, scatter the ashes into the ocean, or cast out stone coffins containing the body. This ritual is also called "藏海" (Hiding in the Sea).
