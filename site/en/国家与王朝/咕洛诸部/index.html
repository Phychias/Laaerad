    GlömerGlömer Tribes¶

    Concept and Etymology¶

    The term GlömerGlömer originally means "by the river" in the Glömezher language. * Origin of the External Name: Initially, the Truskeans used this word to refer to the earliest clan they encountered, named "Glömer". This name became fixed in the Truskean language as a term for barbarians and eventually became the collective name for the northern peoples of Trusk. * Evolution of Self-Identity: Over centuries of interaction between the Glömer people and the Truskeans, especially after the original "Glömer" lineage gradually assimilated and disappeared, the Glömer people themselves gradually accepted this designation.

    Linguistic Differences¶

    The word GlömerGlömer in Glömezher contains the front vowel ö, which contrasts with o, a sound not present in Truskean. Therefore, in Truskean, it is often written as gulomer. The exact cause of this phonetic shift is difficult to confirm; it may have arisen from perceptual differences during the initial contact between the two groups.

    Geography¶

    As an important component of the northeastern corner of the Trusk Empire, the territory of the Glömer people is concentrated in the narrow corridor between the North Sea and the East Sea.

    Topography and Climate¶

    The topography of the Glömer lands is complex and diverse, dominated by rolling mountains that form a unique geographical skeleton.

    • Northern Mountains: Towering mountains reach into the clouds, with perennial snow. They are not only important water conservation areas but also a natural barrier, blocking cold currents from the north, making the climate south of the mountains relatively mild (primarily temperate).
    • River Valleys: Due to the abundance of mountains, flat land is scarce. Rivers meander through the mountain ranges, nourishing the land on both banks. The vast river valleys are the foundation of animal husbandry and the main areas for population settlement and agriculture, considered the lifeline of the national economy.
    • Western Desert: Presents a completely different scene. The vast desert region has a dry climate with scarce precipitation. The surface is mostly sand dunes, gravel plains, and saline-alkali land, but it may contain unknown mineral resources.

    Ecology and Biology¶

    • Vegetation: Dominated by vast grasslands (from valley grasslands to alpine meadows), covering most non-mountainous and non-desert areas. In higher altitude or more northerly regions, cold-tolerant tundra vegetation (mosses, lichens, dwarf shrubs) is distributed.
    • Animals:
      • Livestock: Primarily cattle, sheep, and horses. Horses are used for transportation and military purposes, while cattle and sheep are the foundation of food and economy.
      • Wildlife: Wolf packs are the main predators; eagles dominate the skies; foxes and wild dogs are widely distributed in various environments.

    History¶

    Origins and Legends¶

    The most fundamental origins of the GlömerGlömer people are lost to history due to their nomadic nature and lack of historical records. According to Glömer Legends, the Glömer people were created by the goddess Ramro from her own flesh and blood, and were sheltered by the sacred mountain Karoda during the cataclysm.

    Unification: SajapyomakSajapyomak GuuchobaguGuuchobagu¶

    The first "unified" dynasty of the Glömer people was called the MoswarikeMoswarike Dynasty (Horse Clan Dynasty).

    • Rise: The progenitor GuuchobaguGuuchobagu was originally a survivor who failed in steppe struggles and was enslaved. He used superstition or spiritual power to unite slaves from various tribes during a harsh winter.
    • Seizure of Power: At a major tribal gathering, he killed the drunken elders, shouted "In the name of Latroda!", and promised followers cattle and sheep. He integrated the slave class, subdued the main tribes, and proclaimed himself SajepyomakSajepyomak (Great Elder of All Under Heaven).
    • Rule: This unification was more nominal at the time, with actual power still constrained by the Great Council of EldersLapyomokzhorchi. However, he successfully established a national consciousness for the Glömer people, integrated part of the military, and promoted the slave class.

    Succession Crisis and Southern Flight¶

    GuuchobaguGuuchobagu died without an heir, and his brother OnguchotroOnguchotro succeeded him. After Onguchotro's death, the Great Council of Elders refused to support his son OnguchotronorOnguchotronor, instead electing a collateral line. Onguchotronor resisted but failed and was forced to flee south to [[Trusk]].

    Restoration and Rise of the MoswarikeMoswarike Dynasty¶

    During his exile in Trusk, OnguchotronorOnguchotronor, with the military forces and advanced equipment of a Trusk border lord, counterattacked the Glömer lands and defeated the Great Council of Elders.

    • Reforms: Inspired by the Trusk political system, he established the sacredness and legal legitimacy of the Moswarike family's rule, weakened the power of the Great Council of Elders, transforming it into a consultative body.
    • Magical Revival: He established the JohuzayJohuzay (Priest) system. As this coincided with a period when the "snapshot" was relatively far from the origin, magical effects were significant, and the Glömer people established a mature "Spirit Language" system.
    • Surpassing Trusk: At that time, Trusk was embroiled in the wars between the Heimet and Novaya dynasties, leading to the destruction of texts. The Glömer people's proficiency in Spirit Language surpassed that of the south, laying the groundwork for the future Glömer dynasty that would establish the Trusk Empire.

    Political and Social System¶

    Basic Structure¶

    The political units of the Glömer people are primarily divided by geography and bloodline. Due to their nomadic nature, the structure is loose, making it difficult to form stable political structures.

    • ClanKrota: The basic political unit (e.g., mountain clan, river clan).
    • FamilyShnomca: The basic social unit, practicing patriarchy, collectively owning livestock and tents.
    • Council of EldersPyomakzherchi: The leadership body of each clan. The Great Council of EldersLapyomokzhorchi exists among clans.

    Evolution of Titles¶

    • ElderPyomak: Originally referred to clan leaders, later came to denote nobility.
    • SajepyomakSajepyomak (World Elder): The title for the supreme ruler of the Glömer people. After joining the Trusk Empire, it was used to refer to the emperor.
    • JupyomakJupyomak (Sun Elder): During the imperial period, the title for the Glömer king.

    Religion and Spirit Speakers¶

    The Glömer people do not have a strict religious system; their beliefs are sustained through Spirit SpeakersJohuzay. * Those proficient in the Spirit LanguageJohuz are called Spirit Speakers, forming a loose noble class. * They possess abilities in prophecy, healing, and communicating with spirits. Knowledge is passed down orally, with no written records.

    Economy¶

    • Primarily Nomadic: Relies on herding livestock such as yaks and goats for livelihood, producing fur, tents, clothing, and daily necessities.
    • Limited Agriculture: Some cultivated land exists near rivers, mainly growing cold-resistant edible grain crops.

    Culture¶

    • Tribal Culture: Combines ancestor worship and primitive religious beliefs.
    • Language: Historically, the Glömer people had no strict official language, divided into coastal and inland dialects. The Glömezher language currently documented is based on the southern coastal dialect.
