# History of the Trusk Empire

The history of the **Trusk Empire** is a grand narrative of tribes evolving from division to integration, and from a city-state alliance to a centralized state. Its territory encompassed the nomadic steppes of the north, the core basin of the center, and the maritime city-states of the south, experiencing the rise and fall of several dynasties and the evolution of institutions.

### Geography and Political Composition

The territory of the Trusk Empire primarily consisted of three major regions:

- **Guluo Tribes (Northern Kingdom)**: Comprised of the territories of various tribes of the northern nomadic Guluo people, featuring mainly grasslands and hills.
- **Trusk Region (Central Kingdom)**: The core territory of the empire, later administratively divided into the Kingdoms of Heimet (direct jurisdiction) and Tyesuku, serving as the political and economic center.
- **Laprio (Southern Kingdom)**: Ancient name "Novaya" (meaning "by the sea"), formed by the integration of southern coastal city-states, with a hot and humid climate and developed agriculture and navigation.

## 📜 Early Civilization and Dynastic Changes

### Origins: Land of Artisans and the City-State Era

Based on archaeological and mythological speculation, the various Trusk tribes originated from the Lakme Saumen region and later migrated to the Trusk Basin and surrounding areas. During this period, numerous city-states were scattered across the Trusk region.

- **Trusk City-States**: Leveraging the abundant mineral resources within the basin and the crushed ore deposited by rivers, the Truskeans mastered metal forging technology very early. This technological advantage allowed Trusk city-states to rise rapidly in early wars of annexation.
- **Bagunaluo**: Once a powerful agricultural state around the largest freshwater lake in the Trusk region. After being conquered by Trusk, it solved the supply problems for the Trusk army, laying the foundation for unifying the basin.

After unifying the surrounding areas, the Truskeans did not stop. Through innovations in primitive mechanical technologies like pulleys and iron chains, they undertook large-scale land reclamation and infrastructure construction (canals, roads), establishing the material foundation for subsequent hegemony.

### <b><ruby>Kuileot<rt>Kuileot</rt></ruby></b> "Golden" Dynasty

"Kuileot" in contemporary Truskean is not only a surname but also the etymology for the high nobility <b><ruby>Kuilem<rt>Kuilem</rt></ruby></b>. There are several hypotheses about its origin: one suggests it derives from the word for "gold" (referring to merchants carrying gold artifacts), another posits it is an ancient honorific for "king".

- **Founding Monarch**: Kuileot is the first monarch recorded in inscriptions. During his reign, he amassed great wealth and, through brutal warfare, conquered Heimet, becoming the great river lord from Trusk to the strait.
- **Dynastic Characteristics**: Lasted approximately eight hundred years. Agricultural production accumulated but remained relatively primitive; the political system was stable but lacked clear succession principles (likely a mix of bloodline and abdication).
- **Decline and Fall**: The dynasty's end was marked by large-scale droughts, agricultural collapse, and internal court disputes over bloodline legitimacy, leading to widespread unrest.

### <b><ruby>Heimet<rt>Heimet</rt></ruby></b> First Dynasty

The Heimet region is located south of the Serk Mountains, along the Pernan River, near the bay. By the end of the Kuileot Dynasty, the port of Liewuke in Heimet had become the largest port in the north, with extremely prosperous commerce.

- **Matasioksamen Reforms**: The city lord of Hasit, <b><ruby>Matasioksamen<rt>matasioksamen</rt></ruby></b>, raised an army in the name of the Sea God, ending Kuileot rule. Although he did not personally claim the throne, he effectively controlled the empire and implemented key reforms:
    - **Establishment of Faith**: Systematized the mythological pantheon, elevated the status of Heimet deities, and established the **Heimet-Trusk belief system**.
    - **Infrastructure**: Constructed the "Matasioksamen Road" connecting the strait and the basin, along with coastal ports.
- **Dynasty Founding**: Matasioksamen II, son-in-law of the Kuileot family, ascended the throne, inaugurating the Heimet Dynasty.
- **Achievements**: Agricultural technological innovations solved the food crisis; a preliminary monetary system formed; successfully repelled invasions from the northwestern Andon people and northern pirates.

### Southern Barbarian Invasion: <b><ruby>Novaya<rt>Nowaja</rt></ruby></b> and <b><ruby>Basib<rt>basib</rt></ruby></ruby></b> Dynasties

In the late Heimet Dynasty, with the arrival of the "[Snapshot](../../地理/拉埃拉德.md#魔法)" high phase difference period, the proliferation of "magic" caused social unrest. The Laprio people in the south formed a city-state alliance and, leveraging climatic advantages and accumulated agriculture, surpassed the north in comprehensive national power.

- **Novaya Dynasty**: The Novaya War launched by the Laprio people lasted about twenty years, ultimately capturing Heimet. Its ruling style tended towards "non-interference," with the upper echelons composed of warlords and merchants, lacking a strict aristocratic class.
- **Basib Dynasty**: While the Novaya army was campaigning against the northern Guluo tribes, the long-dormant "Basib" faction staged a coup and seized power. This dynasty lasted about 88 years, during which no major reforms occurred.

## ⚔️ Guluo Ascendancy and Imperial Turmoil

### <b><ruby>Moswarike<rt>Moswarike</rt></ruby></b> Dynasty (Guluo Dynasty)

The tenth great chieftain of the Guluo people, **Tuluhai Ke** (meaning "the belligerent one"), unified the northern tribes. Utilizing the spirit language (Johuza) technology of the high phase difference period, he established a special forces unit focused on spirit language warfare.

- **Conquest Process**: Tuluhai Ke first pledged allegiance to Heimet/Novaya to lull his opponents, then launched a surprise attack when the Basib Dynasty was not yet firmly established. Although initially hampered by a lack of a navy, he ultimately annihilated the enemy forces using the battlefield segmentation abilities of spirit language users and entered Heimet.
- **Governance Characteristics**:
    - **Crude Administration**: Retained the original Trusk tax system, relied on intelligence agencies (Molofa) to control various regions.
    - **Feudal System**: Enfeoffed numerous relatives and meritorious officials, attempting to integrate Trusk institutions.
    - **Cultural Fusion**: The Guluo people began learning Truskean; extensively experimented with spirit language technology.
- **Decline and Fall**: During the magic low tide, the abilities of spirit language users declined, the imperial court became corrupt, and it was eventually overthrown by the Jaudzya family.

### <b><ruby>Jaudzya<rt>Jaolia</rt></ruby></b> Dynasty

The Jaudzya family (originally a warrior family) established a brief rule after overthrowing the Moswarike Dynasty.

- **Failed Centralization**: The first monarch, <b><ruby>Tika<rt>tika</rt></ruby></b> I, attempted to establish a unified government with strong military and political control.
- **Internal Strife**: After Tika's death, his son Yekika, upon seizing power, enfeoffed meritorious officials, undermining the bureaucratic system. After Yekika's death, the state fragmented again.

### <b><ruby>Heimet<rt>Heimet</rt></ruby></b> Second Dynasty and the "Rule of Three Emperors"

The Heimet family restored its rule, ending the chaos of the Jaudzya period.

- **Political Integration**: Implemented tolerant ethnic policies, absorbing the Guluo people through trade and official positions; established a polytheistic system with the Sea God as the supreme ruler, unifying religious identity.
- **Institutional Innovation**: Implemented a mixed system of feudalism and military districts (themes), enfeoffing local chieftains as governors/tusi in border regions.
- **Rule of Three Emperors**: Three consecutive outstanding emperors ruled for a total of forty-eight years, leading to comprehensive recovery in agriculture, handicrafts, and trade, and a thriving society.

### <b><ruby>Tyesuku<rt>Tyesuku</rt></ruby></b> First Dynasty

The third emperor had no heir, and power passed to his nephew, the eldest son of the Prince of Tyesuku (meaning "warm stone"), **Dezhuohei**.

- **Dezhuohei's Reforms**: Divided the empire into large administrative regions, appointed officials for management, strengthening centralization.
- **Territorial Expansion**: Suppressed rebellions in the north and south. Although forced to negotiate peace and arrange a marriage alliance with the Guluo in the north, he significantly expanded maritime territories in the south, discovering Long Island and Mibien.
- **Economic Measures**: Improved coinage, clarified the tax system, encouraged land reclamation in the Laprio region.

### Sunset Invasion and the <b><ruby>Portesdrahu<rt>Portesdrahu</rt></ruby></b> Dynasty

After Dezhuohei's death, the mediocre emperor Heimet·<b><ruby>Vatom<rt>Vatom</rt></ruby></b> I, who was installed, triggered a civil war. Dezhuohei's eldest daughter, the far-married Guluo princess <b><ruby>Vatesukasem<rt>Vatesukasem</rt></ruby></ruby></b> (Sunset Empress), seized the opportunity to lead a Guluo army in a counter-invasion, historically known as the "Sunset Invasion".

- **Portesdrahu Rule**: Vatesukasem's son established the Portesdrahu Dynasty.
    - **Century of Turmoil**: Due to the Guluo people's lack of clear succession laws, frequent struggles for the throne ensued. Power often fell into the hands of powerful ministers or maternal relatives.
    - **Freedom and Prosperity**: The decline of central power unexpectedly brought a relaxed environment for local autonomy. Commerce flourished unprecedentedly, technology (iron plows, waterwheels) advanced, and religious beliefs diversified.
- **Crisis and End**: Long-term laxity led to severe land annexation, and the Guluo garrison became corrupt and degenerate. Ultimately, a collateral branch of the Tyesuku family, **Dezhuohei III**, launched an uprising and captured the capital.

## 🏛️ The True Empire: Tyesuku Second Dynasty

The restoration of the Tyesuku family (Tyesuku Second Dynasty) marked Trusk's true entry into a mature imperial era. The imperial court implemented deep reforms in administration, military, and economy, establishing an efficient centralized system.

### 1. Administrative Reforms

- **Succession Law Established**: Instituted a strict primogeniture succession system, clarifying the order of succession for concubines' sons, brothers, illegitimate children, and women, eliminating legal confusion.
- **Regional Division**:
    - **Kingdom of Heimet (Direct Jurisdiction)**: Directly governed by the emperor or a prince, no enfeoffment, implementing a prefecture-county system to ensure capital security and fiscal independence.
    - **Tyesuku Border Region**: Governed by the King of Tyesuku (a collateral branch of the imperial family), implementing a feudal system to consolidate the western border defense.
    - **Kingdom of Laprio**: Governed by the King of Laprio, with the Annan Protectorate established underneath to manage colonization and diplomacy. The imperial court held ultimate intervention rights over the succession of the King of Laprio.
    - **King of Guluo (Northern Kingdom)**: Established the <b><ruby>Jupyomak<rt>Jupyomak</rt></ruby></ruby></b> (Sun Chieftain) to lead the various clans. Southern clans gradually feudalized, while the north retained the tribal system.

### 2. Military Reforms

- **Central Army**: Established the Imperial Guard directly under the emperor, serving as a check and balance against the Heimet Army led by the Prince of Heimet.
- **Local Armies**: Kingdoms mostly used conscription systems; the Guluo retained tribal militias but under surveillance.

### 3. Economic Policies

- **Currency Unification**: Implemented imperial coinage (copper/silver) in Heimet and core regions, enforcing a single currency tax system.
- **Pluralistic Coexistence**: Considering regional differences, the Guluo region retained barter and tax-in-kind; remote areas allowed tax-in-kind and coexistence of local/underground banks to maintain economic vitality.

Through powerful institutional design, the Tyesuku Second Dynasty successfully forged the three distinct regions of Trusk, Guluo, and Laprio into a unified political entity.
