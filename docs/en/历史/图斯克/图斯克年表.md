# Chronology

The following is the chronology of the Trusk Empire, recording the stories that occurred in Trusk during various historical periods.

## Ancient Era

(3500 BC - 1500 BC)

- **Around 3500 BC**: Ancient Trusk tribes formed in the western mountains, early settlements appeared, and bronze smelting technology began to develop.

- **Around 3000 BC**: Early city-states began to form, and a primitive polytheistic belief system took shape; over a dozen city-states of various sizes emerged in the area of present-day Trusk.

- **3000 BC - 1500 BC**: Wars among city-states, the rise and fall of the [Ancient Trusk](../../地理/图斯克/index.md), Turha, and Bagunalo city-state alliances.
    - 3000 - 2850 BC: The rise of city-states like [Ancient Trusk](../../地理/图斯克/index.md), Bagunalo, Turha, and Pernan along the river.
    - 2850 - 2800 BC: Ancient Trusk maintained peace in its corner, while Bagunalo, Turha, Pernan, and city-states in the Heimet region engaged in warfare; Turha achieved hegemony.
    - 2800 - 2700 BC: Barbarian invasions from the Andon lands; the Bagunalo and Heimet regions formed a city-state alliance to jointly resist the barbarian invasions from Andon.
    - 2700 - 2500 BC: Ancient Trusk conquered the Genara Highlands and established rule; a primitive feudal system formed within Trusk city-states, leading to a differentiation between "city lords" and "lord of all cities"; the Bagunalo alliance dissolved, plunging the region back into warfare; the Heimet region suffered a great flood, descending into darkness.
    - **Around 2450 BC**: Internal strife in Ancient Trusk; three cities from present-day Zigwa jointly invaded, putting Ancient Trusk in crisis. It allied with the Turha people, counterattacked the Zigwa, and captured two cities; the great Bagunalo wars largely ended; early pirates appeared in the Heimet region.
    - 2400 - 2200 BC: Pirates claiming to be followers of the god *Kongdunian* invaded, severely damaging the cities of Turha, Bagunalo, and Zigwa.
    - **Around 2190 BC**: Ancient Trusk joined the war against the pirates and repelled them. However, some generals who converted to the Kongdunian faith during the war rebelled, occupying the Turha lands. Ancient Trusk allied with Bagunalo and other cities to wage war against Turha.
    - **Around 2180 BC**: The *Kongdunian* War ended, and the Trusk city-states entered a relatively stable period of recovery.
    - **2000 BC**: Ancient Trusk successfully conquered Zigwa, Pernan, Genara, Krusofa, and other regions, quickly becoming the largest state in the Trusk region.
    - 2000 - 1900 BC: Internal strife in Ancient Trusk; Andon invaded Pernan.
    - 1900 - 1700 BC: Internal institutional changes in Ancient Trusk; forging technology steadily improved, and farming efficiency increased; city-states in Heimet, Bagunalo, and other regions declined due to intermittent large-scale droughts or floods; scattered pirates occupied coastal cities; Heimet residents crossed the sea to expand territory on the opposite shore of the strait, rebuilding Genara.
    - 1700 - 1500 BC: The曲折 unification of the Ancient Trusk state.

## City-State Period

(1500 BC - 183 BC)

### [Kuileot Dynasty](../../国家与王朝/图斯克帝国/奎列奥王朝.md)

(1500 BC - 865 BC)

- **Around 1500 BC**: After the rise of the Kuileot family, they politically unified Trusk, Bagunalo, and other regions for the first time, marking the formal establishment of the **Kuileot Dynasty**. Subsequently, the Trusk state gradually unified the rest of the Trusk Basin and the Heimet Peninsula, further expanding eastward and southward. It launched military campaigns against the northern city-states of Laprio, eventually forming alliances with them, consolidating the dynasty's rule.

- **Around 1200 BC**: The power of Heimet city-states increased, annexing smaller city-states and contending for power with Kuileot forces, forming separatist regimes.

- **Around 900 BC**: Hasit city lord Matasioksamen I took advantage of the chaos in the Kuileot Dynasty to attack the then Trusk capital, killed the Kuileot king, held his son as a puppet emperor, monopolized power, and moved the capital to Heimet.

### [First Heimet Dynasty](../../国家与王朝/图斯克帝国/黑蔑特王朝.md#黑蔑特第一王朝)

(883 BC - 456 BC)

- **883 BC**: **Matasioksamen II** formally established the First Heimet Dynasty, completed the Matasioksamen Road, and built the Borlasom Port.
    
- **853 BC**: **Matasioksamen III** conquered and annexed Zhaoxiasi, established the harem and the **eunuch system**, marking the雏形 of Trusk court politics.
    
- **845 BC**: The illegitimate son **Boraspon** ascended the throne, conquered Krusofa, built the Tupero fortress, mined gold, bringing the dynasty's military power to its peak.
    
- **830 BC**: **Grusabot** ascended the throne, and the **Visiyufeng family** began an eighty-year era of maternal relatives holding power.
    
- **815 BC**: **Tamog I** assumed personal rule, balancing the power of maternal relatives and princes by supporting the merchant class. Imperial power reached extreme centralization during this period.
    
- **760 BC**: **Aguijio** (the emperor's younger brother) acted as regent, ending the shadow rule of the Visiyufeng family. He ruled for 50 years, launched the "Ruilian Meat Grinder War," and although he recaptured the gold mines twice, he exhausted the state's strength.
    
- **650 BC**: With the throne left vacant due to civil war, the empire entered a 62-year "Aristocratic Republican Period" (rule by seven families). Seven major powers including **Heimet, Visiyufeng, Ruilian, Zhaoxiasi, Basib** formed a senate, leading to the collapse of central authority.
    
- **598 BC**: The political genius **Zhuo Aoxi** exploited family conflicts to abolish the republic and became lifelong "Chief Consul."
    
- **Around 520 BC**: **Zhuo Aoxi II** attempted to implement the "Second Centralization Reforms" but failed and died in exile. The First Heimet Dynasty彻底落幕, the Trusk Empire崩解, entering a power vacuum period of诸侯混战 and the northward advance of the Laprio people.

### [Laprio Dynasty](../../国家与王朝/图斯克帝国/拉普里奥王朝.md)

(455 BC - 278 BC)

- **455 BC**: The Novaya (Laprio) people established the Laprio Alliance in the south and invaded Heimet. The Trusk people also called it the Novaya Dynasty.

### Basib Dynasty

(278 BC - 190 BC)

- **278 BC**: The Basib Dynasty captured Hasit city, expelled the Laprio people, crowned an emperor, establishing the Basib Dynasty, which formed a north-south割据 with Laprio.

- **190 BC**: The Moswarike tribe chieftain <ruby>Guuchobagu<rt>guuchobagu</rt></ruby> of the Guluo people unified the steppe tribes, proclaiming himself "<ruby>Sajapyomak<rt>sajapyomak</rt></ruby>" (meaning Great Elder of the World). The雏形 of the Moswarike Dynasty appeared, and Guluo ethnic consciousness awakened.

## Pre-Imperial Period

(200 BC - 1 AD)

### [Moswarike Dynasty](../../国家与王朝/图斯克帝国/莫斯瓦里克王朝.md)

(183 BC - 71 BC)

- **183 BC**: The great Guluo chieftain Tuluhai launched the "Strait Campaign," conquered Heimet, and established the **Moswarike Dynasty**.

- **173 BC**: The Moswarike Dynasty unified the entire Trusk region except for the Laprio area, then advanced into the Laprio region, beginning a long thirteen-year war, ultimately unifying the entire present-day Trusk region **around 160 BC**.

- **Around 150 BC**: During the reign of <ruby>Onguchotronor<rt>Onguchotronor</rt></ruby>, the priestly status of the <ruby>Johuzay<rt>Johuzay</rt></ruby> (Spirit Speakers) was established, and religious and legal reforms were implemented. This period coincided with the "Snapshot" moving far from the origin, ushering in a golden age for spirit language technology (magic). Coupled with the long-term warfare in the southern Trusk region leading to the destruction of典籍, the Guluo people's spirit language水平 began to surpass that of Trusk, laying the foundation for the later comprehensive rule of the Moswarike Dynasty.

- **Around 80 BC**: A魔法低潮期 caused the Moswarike Dynasty's rule to动摇, with rebels rising everywhere.

### [Jaudzya Dynasty](焦利亚王朝.md)

(78 BC - 48 BC)

- **78 BC**: The Jaudzya family emerged victorious in the war, entered the strait, established the Jaudzya Dynasty, and formed a割据对立 with the Laprio people in the south.

- **48 BC**: Failed reforms of the Jaudzya Dynasty; Trusk fell into internal strife again. A palace coup began in winter, historically known as the **"Long Night Rebellion,"** plunging Trusk into twenty years of割据混战.

### [Second Heimet Dynasty](黑蔑特王朝.md#黑蔑特第二王朝)

(65 BC - 1 BC)

- **65 BC**: The Heimet family launched a coup, establishing the Second Heimet Dynasty.

- **5 BC**: Tyesuku De Zhuohai, as Ansait's right-hand man, began reforms. Later that same year, Ansait died suddenly, and De Zhuohai, as Ansait's brother-in-law, took the stage.

## Early Imperial Period

### [First Tyesuku Dynasty](../../国家与王朝/图斯克帝国/铁苏库王朝.md#铁苏库第一王朝)

(1 AD - 48 AD)

- **1 AD**: Tyesuku De Zhuohai rebelled, captured Hasit city, ended the Second Heimet Dynasty, and established the **First Tyesuku Dynasty**. Calendar reform was implemented, the Trusk AD was adopted, and the legal framework of the Tyesuku Kingdom was formed.

### [Third Heimet Dynasty](../../国家与王朝/图斯克帝国/黑蔑特王朝.md#黑蔑特第三王朝)

(48 AD - 50 (58) AD)

- **48 AD**: Tyesuku De Zhuohai died, the Heimet Dynasty was restored, and Heimet Fatuo I ascended the throne, historically known as the Third Heimet Dynasty.

### [Botesizhuahu Dynasty](../../国家与王朝/图斯克帝国/伯帖斯抓护王朝.md)

(50 (58) AD - 153 AD)

- **50 AD**: The Laprio people rebelled. Simultaneously, Tyesuku Watesukasem, De Zhuohai's eldest daughter who had married into the Guluo, led her husband's family's army to attack Hasit city. Fatuo first quickly defeated the Laprio people in the south, then defeated the Guluo army in the suburbs of Hasit city, forcing the Guluo west of the strait, and entered peace talks with them. At a banquet, Watesukasem arranged for assassins to murder Fatuo and重新入主 Heimet. Mainstream accounts mark this as the beginning of the **Botesizhuahu Dynasty**, though some scholars refer to it as the Second Tyesuku Dynasty.
    
- **58 AD**: Watesukasem's son, **Botesizhuahu Samentim I**, ascended the throne, formally beginning the Botesizhuahu Dynasty. Real power was held by his father, Prince **Botesikou**, and court affairs were chaotic due to迷信灵语占卜.
    
- **58 AD - 150 AD**: The **"Century of Turmoil."** Central authority existed in name only, with frequent changes on the throne, often involving regencies and internal strife. However, due to weakened central control over the regions and宽松 taxation,民间商业 and free trade flourished空前, and culture became多元化.
    
- **Around 150 AD**: Late Botesizhuahu Dynasty, severe land兼并,财政枯竭, and declining military effectiveness of Guluo armies. A巨大彗星划过夜空, seen as an omen of dynastic change.

(153 AD - )
