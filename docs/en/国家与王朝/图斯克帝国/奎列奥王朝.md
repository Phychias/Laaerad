# <ruby>Kuileot<rt>kuileot</rt></ruby> Dynasty

The dynasty established by **Kuileot** lasted for approximately eight hundred years and is referred to by later generations as the **Kuileot Dynasty**. Interestingly, the names of the first few kings who succeeded to the throne were not directly related to **Kuileot**; for example, one was named "The Roast Meat Man (<ruby>Leukkiap<rt>leukkiap</rt></ruby>)". It was only after the fourth king that the name **Kuileot** began to be prefixed to all monarchs' names, and consequently, the dynasty came to be known as **Kuileot**.

The social and economic foundations during this period were relatively primitive. Agricultural productivity was limited, insufficient to sustain a long-lasting and stable rule. Incursions by barbarian tribes occurred from time to time, and the exchange of goods had not yet developed into a mature trade system. Although the core political system was maintained, the inscriptions regarding royal succession are vague, making it impossible to determine whether it was a hereditary system based on bloodline or some form of abdication system. This unstable foundation sowed the seeds for the dynasty's decline.

The end of the **Kuileot Dynasty** stemmed from a combination of internal and external factors. Inscriptions indicate that the dynasty's final period likely experienced a prolonged drought, dealing a heavy blow to the already fragile agricultural base. Simultaneously, doubts arose within the court regarding the legitimacy of the reigning king's bloodline. These internal troubles and external threats ultimately triggered a massive upheaval, leading to the rapid collapse of the **Kuileot Dynasty**.

## Etymology

**Kuileot** is also a concept in [Ancient Truskean](../../../en/概念/图斯克.md), referring both to the first historically documented king in Truskean history and to the dynasty he founded, which lasted about eight hundred years. The word itself holds multiple meanings in the Truskean language and history, potentially referring to a deity or wealth, and it also became a symbol of royal authority.

There are several speculations regarding the etymology of the term **Kuileot**:

-   **Name of a Deity**: One hypothesis suggests that an early city-state conquered by the Truskeans worshipped a deity named **Kuileot**. During the prolonged process of unification, this divine name gradually evolved into a general term for the nobility of that city-state. However, this name does not appear in later Truskean-Heimet pantheons, leaving this hypothesis lacking direct evidence.
-   **Meaning "Gold"**: Another mainstream view posits that in Ancient Truskean, the original meaning of **Kuileot** was "gold". This word might have initially been a general term for merchants who frequently used gold as currency. Some of these merchants later became political strategists, playing a significant role in the wars that unified the Trusk Empire.
-   **Alternative Title for "King"**: Building on the "gold" hypothesis, another interpretation is that **Kuileot** was a form of address for "king", symbolizing "the person who possesses the most gold in the world".

All these theories point to one **fact**: **Kuileot**, during the era of Ancient Truskean city-states, represented an important political force with immense influence, a force that ultimately coalesced into **King Kuileot** himself.

## King Kuileot

**King Kuileot** is the first recorded monarch in Truskean history. Prior to this, Truskean civilization seemingly did not have the habit of recording history, although some scholars speculate that older rulers might have existed, but their records may have been lost due to some catastrophic event.

According to unearthed inscriptions, during his reign, **Kuileot** collected treasures from across the land, issued decrees, and constructed palaces. One of his most significant achievements was launching the decisive **Heimet War**. Although the inscriptions do not detail the specifics of the war, they mention that it "nearly exhausted" the treasures he had amassed, hinting at its brutality. After conquering Heimet, **Kuileot** became the overlord of all city-states from Trusk to the two great rivers of the strait, laying the foundation for the Trusk Empire.
