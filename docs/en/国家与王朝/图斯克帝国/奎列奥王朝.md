## 🏛️ Dynasty Overview

The **Kuileot Dynasty** was established by **Kuileot** and lasted approximately eight hundred years. Although the dynasty is named after its founder, the names of the initial few successors are not directly related to "Kuileot"; one king was even called "the Roast Meat Man (<ruby>Leukkiap<rt>leukkiap</rt></ruby>)." It was only after the fourth king that all monarchs' names were prefixed with "Kuileot," from which the dynasty derived its name.

The socio-economic foundation during this period was relatively primitive. The dynasty's actual control was largely confined to the Truskean Basin, including areas such as Bagnaro, Ancient Truske, and Northern Turha, with weak authority over regions outside the basin. Agricultural production was limited, making it difficult to sustain long-term stable rule; incursions by barbarian tribes were frequent, and material exchange had not yet developed into a mature trade system. The succession system for the throne is ambiguously recorded in inscriptions, making it unclear whether it was hereditary by bloodline or involved other forms of abdication. This instability sowed the seeds for the dynasty's later decline.

The collapse of the dynasty resulted from a combination of internal and external factors. Inscriptional evidence suggests that the late period may have suffered from prolonged drought, severely impacting the already fragile agriculture. Simultaneously, internal court disputes arose questioning the bloodline legitimacy of the reigning king. These internal and external crises ultimately triggered large-scale turmoil, leading to the rapid downfall of the **Kuileot Dynasty**.

## 📚 Etymology

**Kuileot** is a significant concept in [Ancient Truske](../../地理/图斯克/index.md) culture, referring both to the first historically recorded king and the dynasty he founded, which lasted about eight hundred years. The term holds multiple meanings in Truskean history and language, possibly referring to a deity, wealth, and gradually becoming a symbol of royal authority.

Regarding its etymology, the following speculations are prominent:

- **Name of a Deity**: One hypothesis suggests that an early city-state conquered by the Truskeans worshipped a deity named **Kuileot**. During the integration of city-states, the deity's name evolved into a collective term for the nobility of that city-state. However, this deity's name does not appear in later Truskean-Heimet pantheons, lacking direct evidence.
- **Meaning "Gold"**: The mainstream view holds that in Ancient Truskean, **Kuileot** originally meant "gold." The term might have initially referred to merchant groups who commonly traded in gold, some of whom later became political strategists playing key roles in the Truskean unification wars.
- **Epithet for the King**: Building on the "gold" hypothesis, it was further extended as a title for the "king," meaning "the one who possesses the most gold in the world."

All the above interpretations point to the same **fact**: during the era of Ancient Truskean city-states, **Kuileot** represented a political force of significant influence, which ultimately coalesced around **King Kuileot** himself.
