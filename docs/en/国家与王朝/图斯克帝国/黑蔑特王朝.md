# Heimet Dynasty
![](../../../assets/img/Gemini_Generated_Image_a2sv1va2sv1va2sv.png)


## Heimet First Dynasty

Initiated by [Matasioksamen II](马塔肖萨门二世.md) ascending the throne as the son-in-law of the Kuileot family. During this period, the empire achieved significant development in agriculture, commerce, and military affairs. The reforms of [Matasioksamen I](马塔肖萨门一世.md) laid the foundation for the dynasty's rule, improved the tax system, constructed major roads and ports, and successfully repelled invasions from the Andon people and the North Sea pirates.

### List of Monarchs

#### 883 BC [Matasioksamen II](../../人物/黑蔑特/马塔肖萨门二世.md)

Ascended the throne as Kuileot by being the son-in-law of the Kuileot family. The Matasioksamen Highway was completed, the Port of Borsalosom was constructed, checkpoints began to be established throughout the empire, and new taxes were levied on merchants, filling the treasury of Hasit and laying the groundwork for the Trusk Empire's subsequent military campaigns in the north and south.

#### 870 BC Lieukti

Second son of Matasioksamen II, who gained the throne through rebellion. Achieved advantages in the war against Laprio.

#### 868 BC Kansoi

Third son of Matasioksamen II, younger brother of Lieukti. During his reign, he promoted administrative reforms, making court politics more disciplined. Invented the military garrison system, using military management to control border towns and foreign trade.

#### 855 BC Loshula

Eldest son of Kansoi, continued his father's policies, maintaining a peaceful and stable political situation.

#### 853 BC Matasioksamen III

Second son of Loshula, historically assessed as a tyrannical ruler. During his reign, he launched multiple wars against Guluo, conquered the land of Zhaoxiasi, and implemented ethnic massacres. Established the Heimet court's harem system and began castrating prisoners of war and slaves to serve as eunuchs.

#### 845 BC [Boraspon](../../人物/黑蔑特/波拉斯蓬.md)

Illegitimate son of Matasioksamen III, legitimized under the harem system. He was an outstanding military strategist who conquered the land of Krusofa and built the Fortress of Tupero, launched three attacks on the Ruilian land, established the city of Boraspon, and mined the gold there.

During this period, Boraspon spent very little time in Heimet, with most of his time spent in the southern part of the empire, planning and organizing the southern territories. He ordered the construction of a city in the middle reaches of the Krusofa River, naming it Fortress Tupero, meaning "Fortress of Victory." This period saw the political center of Trusk shift southward, while Hasit and the Port of Ikle remained the economic centers of the empire.

#### 835 BC Boraspon II

Eldest son of Boraspon, built the road to the estuary of the Krusofa River but lost the Ruilian land and its gold mines during his rule.

#### 830 BC Grusabot

Boraspon II had no sons, so his nephew Grusabot succeeded him, with his maternal line being the Visyufon family. This marked the beginning of eighty years of maternal relative dominance in the Heimet Dynasty.

#### 822 BC Mapetumat

Younger brother of Grusabot, during his reign, he was deeply embroiled in power struggles between maternal relatives and princes. He was mediocre, and inscriptions provide little record of him.

#### 817 BC Ebumakt

Prince Yepken, cousin of Boraspon II, gained the throne through military achievements. He plotted a rebellion, deposed Mapetumat, and installed Ebumakt, the son of Boraspon's legitimate daughter, as ruler.

#### 816 BC Mapetumat (Restored)

Ebumakt was assassinated after only ten months in power by the maternal relative Visyufon Nottsot. To counterbalance Yepken, Nottsot mobilized the Turha army. Yepken fled to Geqiwa, leading to a stalemate from Fortress Peturo to the land of Krusofa.

#### 815 BC [Tamog](../../人物/黑蔑特/塔莫格.md)

Mapetumat was assassinated by the prince faction, and his young son Tamog ascended the throne. At that time, Nottsot and Yepken reached an agreement to restrain each other. When Tamog assumed personal rule, he supported the merchant class to counterbalance the two major powers and reformed the bureaucratic system in the [Trusk region](../../地理/图斯克/index.md), promoting bureaucrats as confidants and incorporating them into the power balance. During his more than forty-year reign, imperial power was unprecedentedly strengthened, becoming one of the periods of most concentrated imperial authority in Heimet history.

#### 771 BC Tamog II

Sixth son of Tamog, originally named Jilanmie, changed his name after ascending the throne. Continued to promote the construction of the bureaucratic system based on his father's work but lacked his father's skill and boldness; imperial power gradually declined in the mid-to-late period.

#### 760 BC [Agujio](../../人物/黑蔑特/阿贵吉奥.md)

Cousin of Tamog II. Early in his brother's reign, Agujio gained command of the Turha army, the core legion of Trusk. He exploited Tamog II's indecisive character, gradually eliminating the influence of the Visyufon family in the court through a series of purges, ending the family's decades-long shadow rule. Finally, he staged a coup, forcing his brother to grant him full regency powers. However, he waited until his cousin died of illness before formally crowning himself emperor.

During his reign, Agujio demonstrated a strong personal will. To completely solve the financial source problem in the south, he launched the famous "**Ruilian Land Meat Grinder War**." He captured the Ruilian land twice, regained control of the gold mines, and attempted to establish permanent military colonies there. However, due to overly long supply lines and fierce guerrilla resistance from the local natives, both conquests ultimately ended in forced withdrawal due to financial collapse.

Agujio ruled for 50 years. For the first 40 years, he was the absolute dictator of the empire. The imperial power of the Trusk Heimet First Dynasty reached its peak under his rule, and his military achievements earned him the support of his subjects. However, in the last ten years, as he aged and weakened, political power gradually shifted to his political allies and the crown prince. In his later years, he existed more as a symbol.

#### 690 BC Tamog III

Son of Tamog II, nephew of Agujio (some say he was Agujio's adopted son). The historical records of Tamog III's reign represent a significant "gap." According to archaeological excavations and unofficial histories, during his reign, a brutal civil war known as the "Struggle for the Gate" erupted within the Heimet family, a conflict between the direct descendants of Agujio and the direct descendants of Tamog II.

This civil war led to the burning of the Royal Archives in the capital Hasit, resulting in the loss of a large amount of official historical material. Tamog III was likely a mediocre monarch who attempted to balance between the two factions and ultimately died in a palace coup. After his death, due to the decline and loss of prestige of the direct imperial bloodline, various feudal lords refused to pledge allegiance to a single monarch, forcing the state into a **noble republic** status. Although the monarchical title was nominally retained, real power had been stripped away.

#### 650 BC Republican Period (Rule by Seven Families)

During this period, Trusk entered a 62-year era of oligarchic politics. The highest state authority was the "Senate Council" composed of seven major powers**:

1.  **The Heimet family and its branches**: The imperial family split into Heimet, Heimet-Baajalai, and Heimarit, mutually restraining each other.
2.  **The Visyufon family**: Made a comeback as another indispensable force in the imperial center, relying on deep marriage networks and long-standing political power.
3.  **The Ruilian family**: Local power holders controlling southern trade and some mineral resources.
4.  **The Zhaoxiasi family**: Formerly the conquered, now entering the core circle through mercenaries and military force.
5.  **The Basib family**: Emerging major merchant and usury family, controlling the state's financial lifeline.

During this period, centralization collapsed, and each family operated like an independent kingdom within its territory. Although large-scale civil war was avoided, administrative efficiency was extremely low, external expansion completely halted, and Trusk's influence began to shrink from the borders.

#### 598 BC [Zhuoaxi I](../../人物/黑蔑特/卓奥西一世.md)

In the late Republican period, a political genius emerged from the Heimet family—Zhuoaxi. He astutely exploited the conflicts of interest among the other four major families, adopting a strategy of "winning over some, suppressing others."

*   **Family Consolidation**: Through marriage and assassination, he forcibly merged the two largest factions of the Heimet family, unifying the imperial voice. For the third branch (the weaker one) that refused to submit, he relocated the entire clan to the southern border. This Heimet branch later survived tenaciously in the wilderness, becoming the spark for the Yinguhuo restoration centuries later.
*   **Political Compromise**: He reached secret agreements with families like Visyufon and Basib, recognizing their vested interests and hereditary land rights in exchange for their support in abolishing the republic and restoring the monarchy.
*   **Establishment of Dictatorship**: Zhuoaxi did not directly proclaim himself emperor but became the lifelong "Chief Consul," effectively exercising imperial power. He redrew the boundaries of the Trusk core region, forcibly incorporating wavering city-states on the periphery into the territory through military colonization, laying the foundation for the empire's later standard borders.

In his later years, Zhuoaxi became paranoid and muddle-headed, embarking on large-scale palace construction and purging former allies, leading to renewed political turmoil.

#### 520 BC [Zhuoaxi II](../../人物/黑蔑特/卓奥西二世.md)

Eldest son of Zhuoaxi. He was an idealistic monarch who, upon ascending the throne, attempted to revoke the privileges his father had promised to the major families in his later years, promoting the "Second Centralization Reform" to restore the imperial glory of the Tamog era.

However, times had changed. Nourished during the Republican period, families like Visyufon and Basib had grown into unshakable behemoths. Faced with Zhuoaxi II's order to reduce feudal power, the major families quickly formed an alliance and, together with the militarily powerful Zhaoxiasi family, launched the "Senate Coup."

Zhuoaxi II was outnumbered and fled in panic. He was betrayed by former subordinates during his exile and ultimately died on the desolate road to Krusofa.

With the death of Zhuoaxi II, the over 360-year-old Heimet First Dynasty came to a complete end. The Trusk Empire subsequently disintegrated, and the southern foreign Laprio people moved north, beginning the foreign-ruled Novaya-Laprio Dynasty.

## Heimet Second Dynasty

#### 65 BC Yinguhuo

After a long period of dormancy, the Heimet family launched a coup under the leadership of family leader Yinguhuo. Taking advantage of the chaos and decline at the end of the Jaudzya Dynasty, Yinguhuo led the family's private army and defecting imperial guards to storm the palace, ending the rule of the Jaudzya family and formally establishing the Heimet Second Dynasty. After ascending the throne, Yinguhuo quickly quelled the unrest around the capital and issued proclamations to the provinces, announcing the restoration of the Heimet family.

After stabilizing the core ruling area, Yinguhuo adopted a pragmatic attitude towards the nomadic Guluo tribes on the border. After several tentative military conflicts, he signed a treaty with the barbarian chieftain, establishing the border along the line of the great swamp and forest west of the strait, and arranged a marriage between the imperial family and a barbarian princess. This move not only pacified the border troubles but also gave the newly established dynasty precious time for recuperation and reconstruction, allowing it to focus on post-war recovery.

#### 48 BC Laiswi

After Yinguhuo's death, his son Laiswi succeeded. Faced with the cultural fragmentation and religious chaos accompanying the post-war recovery period, Laiswi dedicated himself to spiritual unification. He promoted moderate religious reforms, presided over the compilation of new scriptures, and established a polytheistic system with the Sea God as the supreme ruler, assisted by six main gods, successfully integrating originally conflicting local beliefs and Guluo totems into a unified imperial theological framework.

Politically, Laiswi established a highly inclusive mixed political system. He restored the centralized administrative bureaucracy in the imperial core region, enfeoffed meritorious generals as lords in newly conquered areas under a feudal system, and granted authority to tribal chiefs on the borders, striving to use the improved Sea God religion to maintain border stability. This system effectively balanced various forces at the time, promoted the integration of the Guluo people and the Trusk people, and brought long-term political stability to the empire.

#### 26 BC Oleman

Oleman's reign was the period of greatest national strength for the Second Dynasty, historically known as the "Silver Age" (sometimes the reigns of these three emperors are collectively referred to as the Silver Age). Benefiting from the peace and institutional foundations laid by the first two monarchs, Oleman vigorously promoted economic construction. He repaired ancient water conservancy systems, promoted drought-resistant crops introduced from the Guluo people, significantly increasing grain production. Simultaneously, he encouraged foreign trade; Trusk merchant fleets controlled the surrounding seas, the Trusk silver coin `Guye` became the common currency, and the national treasury was unprecedentedly full.

However, long-term peace and prosperity also bred hidden dangers. In Oleman's later years, the power of local feudal lords and the priestly class became excessively inflated due to long-term accumulation, land annexation was severe, and although central finances were abundant, control over the localities began to decline. When Oleman died, the seemingly prosperous empire was actually undercurrents涌动, with various forces deeply intertwined, setting the stage for the radical reforms of the next ruler.

#### 5 BC Anset

At the end of Oleman's reign, a terrifying plague ended the prosperity of the "Silver Age." The empire's population plummeted to two-thirds of its original size, social structures collapsed, and warlords rose everywhere. Emperor Oleman insisted on inspecting the plague-stricken areas and ultimately contracted the disease and died. The throne was vacant; the eldest son died young, and the second son Hetuke and the deeply favored third son Anset erupted into a succession dispute. In this country where primogeniture was the norm, the taciturn but decisive Hetuke抢先 controlled the capital Hasit city's guards, placing Anset under house arrest in the deep palace. The capital guards and prince's army supporting Anset were投鼠忌器 and had to temporarily compromise.

In these troubled times, the young leader of the [Tyesuku family](../../人物/铁苏库/index.md), [Dezhuohei I](../../人物/铁苏库/德卓黑一世.md), emerged. During this period, he inherited his missing father's position and went to the southern fleet to stabilize the situation. As contact with the center was lost, he independently gathered old forces in the south and established a foothold in eastern Laprio. When Hetuke was besieged by rebels in Hasit city, Dezhuohei led his army north to relieve the siege. He discovered the house-arrested Anset in the palace, conspired with him, and rescued him to the south.

Anset declared his accession in the south, appointed Dezhuohei as chancellor, and to consolidate the political alliance, married his sister to Dezhuohei (rumored that Anset deeply loved his sister, but this painful marriage became the legal basis for Dezhuohei's future seizure of power). The two joined forces to defeat Hetuke in the north, reuniting most of the empire's territory. However, after the victory, the two had significant differences in governance philosophy: Anset favored a looser feudal system, while Dezhuohei advocated centralization and military dictatorship.

In the fifth year of Anset, the矛盾激化. Dezhuohei ultimately plotted the assassination of Anset and, as the son-in-law of the Heimet family, forcibly assumed the empire's highest power. Anset's death marked the substantive demise of the Heimet Second Dynasty. The following year, Dezhuohei abolished the old calendar, established a new dating system, designated as **Trusk Calendar Year 1**, also the first year of the [Tyesuku Dynasty](铁苏库王朝.md), opening a new era belonging to him.

## Heimet Third Dynasty

After the death of Tyesuku Dezhuohei, Heimet Fatuo I came to power, initiating the Heimet Third Dynasty. However, his rule sparked conflicts with the nobility and the forces of Vatsukasem I. Ultimately, Fatuo I was assassinated at the "Sunset Banquet," and the dynasty ended.
