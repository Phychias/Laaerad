# Trusk Empire

<b>Trusk</b> is a regime name frequently mentioned in historical documents, specifically referring to the powerful state established during the late ancient period at the convergence point of the three old continents of Laaerad. Leveraging its unique geographical position controlling both sides of the Heimet Strait, Trusk became a natural hub connecting the eastern and western parts of the continent. It was not only a crucial trade route, facilitating the cross-regional flow of goods, technology, and ideas, but also a melting pot for diverse cultures. In the historical process of the Laaerad world, Trusk, with its irreplaceable strategic position and profound influence, consistently played a pivotal role.

## Civilization 📜

### Language

The official lingua franca of the Trusk Empire is based on the <b>Heimet dialect</b> as the standard pronunciation. This dialect has distinct phonetic features:
- Vowels are pronounced with a wider mouth opening;
- Stress often falls on the penultimate syllable;
- Words frequently end with a falling tone.

Although numerous and intricately related dialects exist within Trusk, they can be broadly categorized into two main branches:
- **Laprio Dialect**: Widely spoken in the southern part of the empire, its vocabulary retains many loanwords from ancient Laprio.
- **Northern Dialects**: Primarily dominated by the aristocratic class of Glömezher origin, they preserve many phonetic characteristics of Glömezher, sounding more rugged and forceful.

For detailed linguistic research and description, please refer to the main entry: [Truskean](图斯克语.md).

### Culture

Overall, Trusk culture exhibits a dualistic yet unified character: it both venerates freedom and a spirit of adventure and places extreme importance on family ties and honor. This unique cultural landscape was shaped by Trusk's specific economic foundation and political structure.

#### A Nation Built on Commerce: The Merchant Culture of Trusk

Early Trusk civilization was based on agriculture and forging, but after the establishment of the <b>Heimet Dynasty</b>, geopolitical advantages and political legacies spurred explosive commercial growth. Due to the special geographical location of the Heimet Strait and the lingering influence of political undercurrents from the First Kuileot Dynasty period, a significant portion of the nobility originated from the merchant class. The shipping and trade system centered on Heimet long served as the empire's primary economic pillar, resulting in <b>"official merchants"</b> occupying a relatively high position within the empire's social structure.

The status of private merchants fluctuated with the political situation. During periods of instability in Novaya and Laprio, merchants not officially registered were often labeled as "pirates" and suppressed by the authorities. This designation often depended on the specific political needs of the ruler.

Nevertheless, the long-term official support for commerce in Trusk, the favorable climatic conditions of [Novaya](../../地理/诺瓦亚.md), the exquisite shipbuilding skills of artisans, and the improved sealed longboats based on "magical" technology collectively provided the material foundation for commercial prosperity. This environment fostered a social ethos that revered free exploration. Folk customs and activities were rich and diverse, often closely related to seafaring and sea god worship.

The Heimet Strait boasts numerous natural deep-water harbors. The seas of the New Nazhi Sea and [Novaya](../../地理/诺瓦亚.md) were forested with ship masts, gathering goods from all over the empire.
- **The Northern Frontier**: Precious sable furs;
- **The Horse Tribes**: Excellent warhorses;
- **Iying**: Exquisite handicrafts;
- **Kuotai**: High-quality ironware.

Prosperous trade not only brought material wealth but also spurred the development of various crafts and art forms, igniting a thirst for culture and knowledge, profoundly driving the evolution of Trusk civilization.

#### Divine Right of Kings: Sole Supremacy on the Maritime World

Trusk's ruling logic was built upon the deep intertwining of religion and aristocratic culture. The religious system centered on <b>Sea God worship</b> and the aristocratic culture meticulously constructed by the Heimet family were two sides of the same coin, forming a web of ideological control over the empire's subjects for a millennium. Entering the imperial era, to serve the mainstream ideology for centralization, it gradually ossified into a political tool for the royal family to control the core regions.

Trusk's theory of <b>"Divine Right of Kings"</b> was built on a core consensus: <b>The legitimate emperor of Trusk will inevitably ascend to become a Drowned God after death.</b>
This theory forms a logical loop: the emperor's divinity stems from his legitimacy, and his legitimacy is, in turn, endorsed by his divinity. Due to the lack of objective third-party criteria, the legitimacy foundation of this theory was not stable, leading to frequent dynastic changes in Trusk's history. This theory is believed to originate from lost Sea God scriptures or to have taken shape during the First Kuileot Dynasty period, but precise textual evidence is no longer available.

Therefore, although Trusk society was relatively open in its ethos, from the city-state era to the imperial era, its social hierarchy remained rigid, especially during the period when the Glömezher took control of Heimet. The legitimacy of early nobles stemmed from actual control over land. With the spread of the Sea God religion, it gradually evolved into being granted by the emperor's divine authority.

Furthermore, the family held a central place in Trusk culture. Society generally valued filial piety and family lineage, long adhering to the bloodline theory of "like father, like son." This concept might be related to the occupational household registration system briefly implemented during the Second Heimet Dynasty period.

## Religion 🔱

### Sea God Religion

The Sea God Religion is the indigenous religion of Trusk, essentially a polytheistic system centered on the worship of sea gods.

*   **Origin and Evolution**: Early inland Trusk city-states primarily worshipped mountain gods and the god of forging. As the city-state alliance expanded its territory to the Heimet Strait and the [Novaya](../../地理/诺瓦亚.md) region, Sea God worship gradually replaced the old gods and dominated the religious system.
*   **Doctrinal Philosophy**: In the religious context, the "sea" is not merely a physical entity but a philosophical concept—symbolizing both the chaos at the world's beginning and metaphorically representing the irreversible flow of fate and nature like water.

#### The Six Main Gods System

The core of Sea God belief consists of six main gods, divided into the "Upper Three Gods" and the "Lower Three Gods." This pantheon likely originated from the deities worshipped by the early city-states that joined the alliance, but their exact prototypes are difficult to verify. The surviving information mainly comes from inscriptions on the Great Road Stele from the Matashok Samen period.

**Upper Three Gods (Sea-Weavers, corresponding to creation myths):**

- **God Soriwar**: Symbolizes the origin of the world. In myth, the world was primordial chaos. God Soriwar awoke first and wove the boundless sea. Presides over **fertility**.
- **God Gyua**: Awoke next and hid half of the sea created by God Soriwar. Presides over **death**.
- **God Noikpi**: "Flipped over" the sea hidden by God Gyua, turning it into land and mountains. Presides over **fate**.

**Lower Three Gods (Sea-Rulers, shadows of the Upper Three Gods):**

The mainstream view holds that the Lower Three Gods are either co-children or "shadows" of the Upper Three Gods. Another theory suggests they are manifestations of God Noikpi.

- **God Pwaso**: Governs shallow seas, mudflats, and beaches. Presides over **warfare**.
- **God Taciap**: Governs the deep sea and weather. Presides over **journeys**.
- **God Ugwat**: Governs rivers and land. Presides over **harvest**.

After the Third Heimet Dynasty, the royal family standardized religious reforms, establishing the specific domains and sacrificial rituals for each deity and creating a dedicated religious calendar.

#### Other Beliefs

- **Nature Deities**: Sun God, Moon God, Wind God, etc., as representatives of natural elements, were incorporated into the Sea God system, each subordinate to a corresponding main god. Some Laprio deities were also absorbed.
- **Drowned God Worship**: This is an extension of Sea God belief. The Truskeans believed that those who died at sea and "returned" had ascended to godhood. The names of these deceased were recorded in divine registers. Therefore, the mainstream Trusk funeral custom is <b>sea burial</b> (also called "sea-hiding"), where ashes are scattered into the sea or stone coffins are sunk into the deep sea, thus returning to divinity.
