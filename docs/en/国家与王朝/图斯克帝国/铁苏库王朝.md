## Tyesuku Second Dynasty

The Tyesuku Second Dynasty was established after [Tyesuku](docs/zh/人物/铁苏库/index.md)'s [Dezhuohei III](德卓黑三世.md) led an uprising and captured the imperial capital. This dynasty implemented comprehensive reforms to the empire's administrative, military, and economic systems. It clarified the system of primogeniture and stipulated that territories would revert to the central government if a lord died without an heir. Administratively, the regions near the capital [Heimet](../../地理与区域/图斯克/黑蔑特/index.md) were designated as the [Heimet Principality](../../地理与区域/图斯克/黑蔑特/index.md#黑蔑特亲王), governed by the emperor or a trusted imperial prince serving as the *Prince of Heimet*, implementing unified administrative management; the old territories of [Tyesuku](docs/zh/人物/铁苏库/index.md) were established as the Kingdom of Tyesuku, governed by a collateral line serving as the [King of Tyesuku](docs/zh/人物/铁苏库/index.md), implementing a feudal system; the [Laprio Region](../../地理与区域/拉普里奥/index.md#拉普里奥地区) was administered by the [King of Laprio](../../地理与区域/拉普里奥/index.md), and the Annan Administrative Office was established in the south to manage colonial affairs; the King of Gulo led the various clans, maintaining tribal or early feudal systems. Militarily, military authority in the capital was separated; apart from the military in [index](docs/zh/国家与王朝/咕洛诸部/index.md) retaining the original tribal system, each kingdom established a standing army. The [Heimet Region](../../地理与区域/图斯克/黑蔑特/index.md) was completely dominated by the standing army, while other regions still relied mainly on feudal levies. Economically, Tusk society, having experienced turmoil, had lost its unified monetary system. The imperial court resumed minting coins in the [Heimet Region](../../地理与区域/图斯克/黑蔑特/index.md), implementing a **single monetary tax system** and simultaneously **taxing commercial transactions**; in [index](docs/zh/国家与王朝/咕洛诸部/index.md), taxes were collected in the form of **material exchange**; in other regions, due to years of continuous turmoil, the monetary system had collapsed for many years, and **taxes in kind** were predominant. At this time, [[Tusk]], despite varying government forms and differing customs and cultures across regions, finally began to be connected under a unified, centralized government, marking the formation of the "true empire."
