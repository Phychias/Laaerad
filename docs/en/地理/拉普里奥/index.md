<b><ruby>Laprio<rt>Laprio</rt></ruby></b> is a composite concept in the southern part of the Laaerad Old Continent, encompassing a specific geographical region, historical state entity, cultural sphere, and language branch. This concept originally stemmed from the early geographical divisions of the Trusk Empire. With the evolution of administrative structures, it gradually solidified into the core political entity in the southern part of the empire—the <b>Kingdom of Laprio</b>.

## Geography and Products 🌾

The **Laprio region** is located in the vast subtropical area south of Tupero in the [Trusk region](../图斯克/index.md). Its geographical boundaries are clearly defined: to the west by the edge of the Great Swamp, to the northwest bordering the Crusofa Highlands, and to the northeast by the natural barrier of the Geziwa Corridor.

The region enjoys a warm and humid climate with excellent hydrothermal conditions. As early as the late period of the [First Heimet Dynasty](黑蔑特王朝.md#黑蔑特第一王朝), with the introduction of advanced deep-plowing tools and irrigation machinery from the north, Laprio's agricultural model rapidly shifted from extensive to intensive cultivation. To this day, the region has become the area with the highest average grain yield per mu within the empire. It is also a major producer of sugarcane, spices, and subtropical fruits, earning the reputation as the empire's "<b>Southern Granary</b>".

## Historical Development 📜

### Tribes and Early City-States

During the ancient Trusk period, constrained by geographical barriers such as dense forests and swamps, the Laprio region had fragmented terrain and failed to form a unified centralized government. Various tribes mostly settled along river valleys or coastlines, with poor transportation and long existing in a loose tribal confederation state.

### Period of Maritime Hegemony

The embryonic form of Laprio as a political concept originated from the struggle for control of the **Laprio Strait**. During the Trusk conquest of the Bagunaluo period, a decisive battle erupted on both sides of the strait: one side was the <b><ruby>La Valau<rt>La Valau</rt></ruby></b> family forces that rose through shipping, and the other side was the city-states controlling mineral resources. Ultimately, the La Valau side, which commanded sea power, emerged victorious, establishing a maritime regime that viewed the Novaya Sea as an internal lake.

This regime once expanded northward to establish outposts in the Zhaoxiasi lands, controlling the vital north-south trade routes of the time. However, this early glory was completely destroyed in the subsequent "Kongdunian Invasion," with major cities reduced to ruins and the civilizational process forcibly interrupted.

### Imperial Tug-of-War Period

After the collapse of the Novaya Dynasty of the Trusk Empire, the succeeding Basib Dynasty briefly claimed sovereignty over Laprio but failed to establish effective rule, quickly losing control in the face of the southward advance of the Guluo people. During the Moswarike Dynasty period, the son of the famous general Tuluhai led troops to capture Laprio again. However, considering the complex tribal relations in the area, they only adopted a policy of loose rein, without establishing formal administrative institutions.

### Establishment of the Administrative Division and Kingdom

During the "Rule of Three Emperors" period of the Second Heimet Dynasty, as the empire's national power recovered, the imperial court regained control of important towns such as Ruilian and Krisa. Subsequently, the empire established the "Laprio Administrative Division" here, formalizing the tribute obligations of local nobles through contracts. This marked Laprio's official status as an administrative entity within the empire.

[Dezhuohei I](德卓黑一世.md) rose to prominence during the chaos of the Great Plague in the year 5 Before. As the commander of the Southern Fleet at the time, he took advantage of the paralysis of the imperial center. He successively captured Krisa, Genala, and Basadi, and besieged the Laprio territory, thoroughly integrating the southern forces. During the confrontation with the northern Hetuke regime, Dezhuohei used Laprio as a strategic rear area, vigorously promoting the popularization of the Truskean language and policies of ethnic integration to consolidate military and tax resources.

With the establishment of the Tyesuku Dynasty, Dezhuohei formally established the <b>Kingdom of Laprio</b> as an autonomous kingdom within the empire. He enfeoffed meritorious officials and local surrendered powerful clans, thus forming the current political landscape.

## Economic Fortress: The Laprio Strait ⚓

Located at the southern tip of [Novaya](../诺瓦亚.md), the Laprio Strait is the strategic throat connecting the inland sea with the outer ocean. Compared to the northern Heimet Port, the port depth conditions of the Laprio Strait are slightly inferior. However, its unique subtropical climate gives the port the advantage of being ice-free year-round. Coupled with the surrounding hinterland's abundant production of high-value-added goods such as timber, spices, and dyes, its economic value is extremely high.

Currently, strait trade is mainly controlled by several ancient merchant guilds, most of which have centuries-old family heritage backgrounds.

## Society and Culture

### Laprio People and Language

The mainstream ethnic group residing here is called the <b>Laprio people</b>. Due to multiple historical conquests by northern regimes and the integration of southern indigenous ancestry, their physical characteristics combine features from both regions.

The local lingua franca is the **Laprio dialect**, a variant of Truskean that incorporates a large number of ancient Laprio vocabulary. It retains many ancient pronunciation habits and a unique honorific system.
