# <b><ruby>图斯克<rt>Trusk</rt></ruby></b>

<b><ruby>图斯克<rt>Trusk</rt></ruby></b> is a polysemous concept. It is a proper name referring geographically to the **Trusk Basin** and its surrounding areas, a historical term for the **Trusk Empire** (and its predecessors and successors), and also a general term for the unique language, culture, and ethnic groups of this region.

As a geopolitical concept, Trusk is located at the geometric center of the **Laaerad** continent. Thanks to its uniquely "centralized" geographical structure—a fertile inland basin surrounded by rugged mountains and strategic straits—this region has become the most densely populated, agriculturally developed, and militarily powerful core area on the continent, hailed as the "Cornerstone of the Empire."

## 1 Etymology

The term "Trusk" originates from the Straits Truskean word *trusk*.

- **Root Tracing**: Its older root can be traced back to the proto-language *tʰro*, meaning "to make" or "to forge."
- **Meaning Evolution**: This etymology reflects the significant early historical advantages of the region's ancestors in shipbuilding technology and metal smelting. As the civilization expanded, "Trusk" gradually evolved from "Land of the Makers" to a general term for the entire core civilization sphere.

## 2 Geographical Overview 🌍

The Trusk region is a relatively independent geographical unit in the central part of the eastern continent, presenting an overall "mountains on three sides, sea on one side" enclosed pattern. This topography not only creates a unique ecosystem but also provides natural geographical boundaries for political unification.

### 2.1 Geographical Boundaries

Trusk commands the crossroads of continental transportation, serving as a natural hub connecting north-south and east-west:

- **Northern Boundary (Maritime Frontier)**: Bordered by the North Sea to the north, with a rugged coastline. Particularly, the strategically vital **Heimet Strait** is the key channel controlling trade between the North Sea and the South Sea.
- **Southern Boundary (Natural Barrier)**: Bounded by the towering and precipitous [Guduk-Athesa Mountains](../古督卡-阿瑟萨山脉.md), this is the region's most solid southern defense line, separating it from the [Laprio](../拉普里奥/index.md) region.
- **Western Boundary (Mineral Highlands)**: The **Tyesuku Mountains** form the western boundary, blocking desert winds and sands from the west, and are also an important resource-producing area.
- **Eastern Boundary (Rainforest Edge)**: The boundary extends to the edge of the tropical rainforests of [Anioksait](安纽科萨.md), a transitional zone east of the Pernan River delta where the climate gradually becomes hot and humid.

### 2.2 Topography

The region's topographic features can be summarized as "one basin, one river, two walls, one coast."

- **Trusk Central Basin**: The main core of the region. This is a vast lowland plain formed by long-term river alluviation, with slightly alkaline, extremely fertile soil. As the "Granary of the Empire," it supports a large population and urban clusters.
- **Pernan River System**: Originating in the southern Guduk Mountains, it flows from south to north through the entire basin and eventually empties into the Novaya Sea. This river system has abundant water but significant seasonal variations. Its middle and lower reaches are navigable year-round, serving as the economic lifeline connecting inland resources to coastal ports.
- **Peripheral Mountains**:
    - **Southern Region**: The terrain is fragmented with crisscrossing canyons. The southwestern edge gradually transitions into the golden **Great Desert**, often subject to sandstorms.
- **Heimet Coast**: A subsidence-type coast with alternating capes and bays, forming numerous natural deep-water harbors.

## 3 Climate and Ecology ☀️

### 3.1 Climate Characteristics

The Trusk core region has a typical **Novaya Sea Climate** (similar to Mediterranean climate / rain and heat occur in different seasons), but significant differences exist in the peripheral zones.

- **Summer (Dry Season)**: Controlled by continental high pressure, it is hot, dry, and sunny, extremely conducive to wheat grain filling and sugar accumulation in grapes and olives.
- **Winter (Rainy Season)**: Influenced by maritime westerlies, the climate is mild and rainy, rivers do not freeze, suitable for growing crops like winter wheat.
- **Local Variations**: The north exhibits temperate monsoon characteristics influenced by cold currents; the southeast, closer to the subtropics, experiences frequent thunderstorms and jungles.

### 3.2 Natural Resources

- **Vegetation**: The core region mainly cultivates durum wheat, barley, grapes, olives, and figs. The mountainous areas are home to large quantities of ironwood and oak, excellent raw materials for shipbuilding and metallurgy.
- **Animals**: Livestock primarily include chickens, ducks, sheep, and pigs. Draft animals are the **Moluo** (adapted to arid land) and the **Ruiba** (endurance cattle). The Novaya Sea is rich in cod, tuna, and the unique **Heimet Purple Shell**.
- **Minerals**: The Tyesuku region contains high-grade magnetite and silver ore (raw materials for Trusk steel), while the southern Ruilian region is rich in gold deposits.
