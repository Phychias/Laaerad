# <ruby>Heimet<rt>heimet</rt></ruby>

**Heimet** (Ancient Truskean: *heimet*) holds multiple meanings in the history of the Laaerad continent, referring both to an important geographical region and city, as well as representing an influential family and the various dynasties established by this family. It occupies a central position in the rise and fall of the Trusk Empire.

## Heimet <ruby>Region<rt>layasli</rt></ruby>

The **Heimet Region** refers to the vast geographical area and cultural center with the city at its core. Throughout the history of the Trusk Empire, Heimet consistently played a pivotal role, serving as the heart of commerce, politics, and military affairs.

The Heimet Region is located in the very center of the eastern Trusk continent, bordering the [Novaya](../../诺瓦亚.md), and commands the [Heimet <ruby>Strait<rt>miela</rt></ruby>](#黑蔑特<ruby>海峡<rt>miela</rt></ruby>) which connects the [Novaya](../../诺瓦亚.md) with the North Sea. Its core area consists mainly of plains and hills near [[Hasit City]], with coastal areas being largely plains suitable for cultivation, making it one of the most fertile regions on the continent.

## Etymology

"*heimet*" originates from [[Proto-Laaeradic]], composed of the root *heim* and the locative suffix *-met*, meaning "in the place where the sun rises." It is widely accepted in academia that the Heimet Region's geographical location at the easternmost part of the [Trusk Region](../index.md) is the reason why the ancient Truskeans named this place Heimet.

### Historical Development

#### City-State Era

The earliest Hasit city-state was located north of Mount Serk but was destroyed by a flood. The later Hasit City was rebuilt south of Mount Serk, along the Pernan River, and near Heimet Bay. Due to its superior geographical location, Heimet gradually developed into the largest port in the north, achieving unprecedented commercial prosperity, which provided a solid economic foundation for its future rise.

#### First Heimet Dynasty

During the late Kuileot Dynasty, **Matasioksamen** led the [[index|Heimet]] army in an uprising, overthrew the old dynasty, and established the [First Heimet Dynasty](黑蔑特王朝.md#黑蔑特第一王朝). Thereafter, [[index|Heimet]] became the capital and power center of the Trusk Empire. The [[Matasioksamen Road]] built by Matasioksamen connected the Heimet Strait with the [[../index|Trusk Basin]], greatly facilitating transportation and trade throughout the empire.

#### Imperial Era

During the reforms of the Second Tyesuku Dynasty, the Heimet Region was established as the **Kingdom of Heimet**. It was governed by the **Prince of Heimet**, a position held by the emperor or a close confidant, and was the core territory of the empire. Taxes from this region were managed directly by the imperial court, and its armies (the **Heimet Army** and the **Imperial Guard**) were controlled by the Prince and the emperor respectively, thereby consolidating central authority. As the capital, Heimet gathered goods, culture, and technology from all over the empire, becoming the center of civilization.

### Culture and Economy

The Heimet Region is the birthplace and center of Truskean commercial culture. Its excellent port conditions, advanced shipbuilding technology, and "magic"-based shipping technology made it a bridge connecting East and West. Various goods were distributed here, fostering a spirit that valued freedom, adventure, and exploration.

Furthermore, Heimet was also the center of Truskean **religious culture**. Matasioksamen carried out religious reforms here, elevating the status of **Sea God worship** and establishing a polytheistic system centered on the Sea God and the six main deities, aiming to unify the ideologies of the various city-states. Throughout the Imperial Era, Heimet was the dissemination center for the Sea God religion, and its concept of the divine right of kings also profoundly influenced the political legitimacy of Trusk.

## Heimet <ruby>Strait<rt>miela</rt></ruby>

The most prominent geographical feature of **Heimet** is the **Heimet Strait**, a strategic waterway connecting the [Novaya](../../诺瓦亚.md) and the North Sea. By controlling the strait, the imperial families and ruling classes of various dynasties held the most crucial transportation hub on the continent, laying the foundation for their commercial and military hegemony and the long-term stability of the state.

The Heimet Strait connects the [Novaya](../../诺瓦亚.md) and the North Sea, becoming a vital hub for Trusk to advance into the North Sea and retreat to the [Novaya](../../诺瓦亚.md). The Heimet Strait is also the only strait on the [Novaya](../../诺瓦亚.md) that possesses a deep-water port unaffected by ocean currents and never freezing; the southern Laprio Strait, while having a longer coastline, is subject to relatively severe ocean currents, frequent storms, and its deep-water area is comparatively insufficient. Such unique geographical advantages established Heimet's absolutely central position throughout the ocean, especially on the [Novaya](../../诺瓦亚.md).

## Prince of Heimet

The Prince of Heimet was established by Dezhorhei III and was responsible for governing the administrative district near the capital. Unlike other regions of the empire which used a governor system or a feudal system for administration, the Principality of Heimet was primarily based on a bureaucratic system. Simultaneously, the Prince of Heimet was responsible for commanding the Capital Guard.
