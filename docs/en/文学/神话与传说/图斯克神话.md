## 1 Overview

Tusk Mythology is a complete theological system formed during the period of <b><ruby>Matasioksamen I<rt>Matashosamen I</rt></ruby></b>. This system has distinct genealogical characteristics, tightly integrating primitive tribal beliefs, nature worship, and imperial political order. The mythological texts mainly consist of six parts, collectively known as the "Sea Canon," which elaborates on the creation of the universe, wars among the gods, ancient heroic epics, and religious laws.

## 2 Creation and the Gods 📜

This section is the cornerstone of the mythological system, explaining the origin of the world and the distribution of divine authority.

### 2.1 The Book of Creation
The *Book of Creation* primarily narrates the process of world creation and the mechanism of the gods' birth. In the cosmology of Tusk Mythology, the gods did not appear out of nothing but played specific and crucial roles in the creation process.

This book establishes the core divine pantheon of Tusk Mythology, namely the "Six Main Gods" system:
- **The Upper Three Gods**:
    - <b><ruby>God Soriwar<rt>The Weaver</rt></ruby></b>
    - <b><ruby>God Gyua<rt>The Hidden / Void</rt></ruby></b> (also known as the God of the Empty Sea)
    - <b><ruby>God Noikpi<rt>The Turner</rt></ruby></b>
- **The Lower Three Gods**:
    - <b><ruby>God Taciap<rt>The Separator</rt></ruby></b>
    - <b><ruby>God Pwaso<rt>The Painter</rt></ruby></b>
    - <b><ruby>God Ugwat<rt>The Extender</rt></ruby></b>
- **Secondary Gods**: Other deities ranked below the Six Main Gods.

### 2.2 The Book of Gods
The *Book of Gods* records in detail the history of divine power transitions and the wars that established order:
- **The War of the Upper Three Gods**: Chronicles the ancient conflicts among God Soriwar, God Gyua, and God Noikpi. The war ultimately ended with God Soriwar's retreat into seclusion and a metaphysical balance reached between God Gyua and God Noikpi.
- **The Rebellion of the Lower Three Gods**: God Taciap, God Pwaso, and God Ugwat once conspired to overthrow the rule of the Upper Three Gods but were ultimately suppressed single-handedly by God Noikpi.
- **Establishment of Order**: After the war, God Noikpi redefined the world order, strictly delineating the domains and boundaries of God Taciap, God Pwaso, and God Ugwat.

## 3 Heroes and Epics ⚔️

This section includes the *Book of Jilanme* and the *Book of Latroda*. Through legends of demigod heroes, it reflects the rise and fall of ancient kingdoms and the evolution of the relationship between gods and humans.

### 3.1 The Book of Jilanme
This volume tells the legendary life of the ancient chieftain <b><ruby>Jilanme<rt>Jilanme</rt></ruby></b> and the great kingdom he established.

- **Source of Power**: Jilanme received divine grace for assisting God Noikpi in war. He formed alliances with God Taciap (mountains) and God Ugwat (sea), gaining the great power to command mountains and oceans. However, being despised by God Pwaso, he was cursed never to rest in the "land between sea and water" (tidal flats).
- **Rise and Fall of the Kingdom**: Jilanme established a magnificent kingdom connecting heaven and earth, ruling over all things. However, heresy emerged among his people, who abandoned God Noikpi and turned to worship God Gyua.
- **The Hero's Fall**: Jilanme attempted to eradicate the heresy, but the power God Gyua bestowed upon his followers was beyond reason. Jilanme personally led the expedition but fell into an illusion during the chase and ultimately died on the tidal flats where he could not rest.
- **Current State of Research**: Although the scriptures detail the divine-human interactions and historical rise and fall of this kingdom, modern historiography has yet to identify its true historical prototype.

### 3.2 The Book of Latroda
Also known as the *Book of Rivers and Mountains*, it was compiled during the <b><ruby>Moswarike Dynasty<rt>Moswalik Dynasty</rt></ruby></b> under the direction of the Great Chieftain Tuluhai. Its core purpose was to integrate the beliefs of the conquered <b><ruby>Gulo<rt>Gulo</rt></ruby></b> barbarians into orthodox theology.

- **The Tragedy of Latroda**: The protagonist Latroda has an obscure origin. Originally a devout follower of God Noikpi, he was granted the ability to "briefly govern the flow of time" for his meritorious service to the god. However, due to his greed for power and attempt to possess time eternally, he was executed by God Noikpi.
    - **Mythological Geographical Explanation**: After death, Latroda transformed into the ceaselessly flowing great river in the north (symbolizing time that never stops). His wife, attempting to resurrect him, was cursed and turned into the solidified sacred mountain by the river (the holy land of the Gulo people).
- **The Reign of Lasvira**: Latroda's brother, Lasvira, usurped the throne and was initially tyrannical. However, because he showed extreme obedience and piety to God Noikpi, he ultimately received the god's forgiveness and blessing.
- **Theological Implication**: This book established the political dogma that "obedience to divine will grants legitimacy."

## 4 History and Evolution

### 4.1 The Book of Kongdunian
This volume is often appended to the *Book of Gods* and is called the <b><ruby>Book of Clay and Blood<rt>Book of Clay and Blood</rt></ruby></b>. It is not a theological revelation but a historical retrospective of the "Era of City-State Warfare," revealing the isomorphic relationship between the Tusk Unification Wars and the establishment of the divine pantheon.

- **Historical Background**: Records the centuries-long warfare among various city-states after the end of the mythological age and before the empire's establishment. At that time, the divine pantheon was chaotic, with each city-state worshipping its own patron god (e.g., the God of Wealth in Bagunaluo, the God of Might in Turha).
- **The Kongdunian Rebellion**:
    - **External Enemy**: The "Kongdunian" pirates from the sea landed, using heretical arts that violated the rules of warp and weft, leading to the corruption and betrayal of the Turha city-state.
    - **Counterattack**: At the critical moment of survival, the ancient Tusk lords descended from the high mountains, united the remaining forces, and launched the "War of Divine Rectification" on the plains, ultimately defeating the corrupt ones and the pirates.
- **Religious Syncretism (Formation of the Pantheon)**:
    - After the war, the ancient Tusk people absorbed the **River Spirit** of Bagunaluo, the **Light and Shadow Spirit** of Peirnan, and the **Mountain Spirit** of Genala into the orthodox faith but demoted them to the **Lower Three Gods** under the jurisdiction of God Noikpi.
    - This process symbolized the absolute sovereignty of God Noikpi (the Observer/Definer) over the gods of natural phenomena, establishing the final form of Tusk polytheism.

## 5 Law and Holy Teachings ⚖️

### 5.1 The Book of Holy Teachings
The final chapter of the *Sea Canon*, primarily compiled over several centuries after the empire's establishment by high priests and court historians.

- **Acts of the Saints**: Records the deeds of mortals regarded as executors of divine will.
    - **Saint Bolaspeng**: Born in the Heimet Dynasty, possessed the divine eye to see through the "cracks between reality and illusion." At the Battle of Tupero, he saw through the illusion of the city wall, causing its collapse; in the Ruilian Wasteland, he saw through the golden skeleton beneath the earth, leading to the discovery of a gold mine.
    - **Saint Tamog**: Blessed by God Soriwar, able to weave "stability" into reality through dense warp and weft, rendering any assassination attempt (a violent change) against him ineffective.
- **The Commandments of Matasioksamen**:
    - **The First Ten Commandments (Secular Law / Law of Weaving)**: Common moral precepts, such as protecting water sources, prohibiting wanton killing, and keeping oaths. Aimed at maintaining the warp-and-weft order of society, they are the ethical cornerstone of Tusk society.
    - **The Latter Ten Commandments (Hidden Law / Law of the Hidden Sea)**: Starting from the eleventh, the content becomes obscure and bizarre, involving spiritual language and occult taboos. This part is usually secretly transmitted only among the higher echelons of the church and has gradually been forgotten in secular society.

## 6 Partial Original Text

### 6.1 Genesis

In the beginning of chaos, there was no distinction between up and down, front and back, left and right; clarity and turbidity were one, cold and heat were one, love and hate were one.

The first was Soriwap, who found the warp and weft from the pervasive chaos and wove the boundless sea. He used His spine as the warp, His nerves as the weft; seawater was the thread and also His blood, hence it is salty.

Boundless sea, without wind or waves, no distinction above the sea, no distinction below the sea.

Coming from the sea, returning to the sea, thus the second emerged from the sea.

Half of the sea was hidden, then towering waves surged, and from the waves emerged the third.

Soriwap said, the sea is woven, warp and weft, salt and water, should not be separated, should follow the way of the sea, the way is there.

The second said, the woven sea is too vast, infinite and endless, endless and infinite, present where it should be, should be where it is present. Warp and weft wove everything, everything without distinction between clarity and turbidity, cold and heat, up and down, cause and effect, love and hate. You have eternal life, wove such a vast sea, yet you yourself cannot fully traverse it. Thus I was born where you cannot see, in the sea you wove, in the sea you traversed, following the way of the sea, transcending the way of the sea. Hiding the sea was not my will, but the way transcended itself, so it had to hide itself, abandoning me.

The third said, you hid the sea, then the non-sea outside the sea appeared. The sea is the non-sea, the non-sea is also the sea. The sea turns over to become non-sea, the non-sea turns over to become sea. I am neither sea nor non-sea; I am both sea and non-sea. The weaver of the sea is one, the hider of the sea is two; I may be three, but I can also be non-three. Three can contain sea and non-sea, this is also the way.

The weaver of the sea, the hider of the sea, and the turner of the sea thus began to argue, unable to cease. The turner could deceive the weaver and the hider, but if the weaver and hider vanished, the turner would also vanish.

The weaver eventually gradually faded His form, endlessly weaving the sea in infinity; the hider continued to hide the sea, He hid in the hidden sea, so the turner could not find Him. The turner thus generated four from three, five from four, six from five. All that was infinite and endless gained visible finitude; all that was undifferentiated between clarity and turbidity gained distinguishable clarity and turbidity; all that was undifferentiated between cold and heat gained distinguishable cold and heat. All that could move, be seen, be heard, be felt, change was abandoned by the way in the churning of the sea.

God Noikpi saw emptiness and called the void to fill it, thus came substance. Void fire spread in the emptiness, God Noikpi carved in the fire for three hundred million years, and there came a sun with both void and substance.

The fourth and the fifth, God Noikpi called them to hold the sun. The fourth threw up the sun, the fifth failed to catch the sun, it came to the sixth, who wanted to hide the sun, to follow a way. God Noikpi said this conforms to my way, you may follow such a way. Thus night appeared.

God Noikpi saw the emptiness of night and again called the fifth who failed to catch the sun to fill it. The fifth said, take one of my eyes, the fifth's eyes one void one substance; thus the sky gained two moons, one visible, one invisible.

Day and night began to alternate, thus wind arose, water flowed, grass grew, fire burned, beasts emerged. The sixth, when not tending the sun, created men and women in the likeness of his brothers; and created other beings in the likeness of God Gyua, God Soriwar, and God Noikpi. God Noikpi thought this conformed to His way, but forbade the sixth from creating beings in His likeness, because He is both being and non-being, both void and substance, the sixth could not make such a thing.

The sixth could not make what conformed to the way, but he wanted to make what did not conform to the way, so he begged the fifth. The fifth again offered a piece of skin from His jaw. The sixth thus created a kind of being in the likeness of God Noikpi.

All things, all people, all gods thus had their positions, had their belonging. This was the first day, and also the last day.
