**Matasioksamen II**, the de jure founder of the First Heimet Dynasty. He was not born into royalty but was the heir of the most powerful commercial family of the time—the Heimet family. He married the last princess of the Kuileot Dynasty through a matrilineal marriage, thus completing the transition from "merchant magnate" to "emperor."

## Biography

* **A Merchant's Wisdom**: Unlike traditional conquerors, Matasioksamen II governed the nation with a merchant's shrewdness. He understood that sheer military force could not sustain a vast territory, so he implemented the famous "Toll Tax Law." This was seen as an act of greed at the time, but history proved that this steady stream of wealth financed the empire's military expansion for the next century.
* **The Empire's Veins**: His greatest legacy is the over 800-kilometer-long "Matasioksamen Highway." This road not only connected the capital to the frontiers but also allowed troop movements to be more than three times faster than those of the surrounding barbarian tribes. Historians note: "The warhorses of the Heimet Dynasty ran on the stones of Matasioksamen."
* **The Port of Borlasom**: To monopolize maritime trade, he expanded the deep-water Port of Borlasom. In his later years, he often sat in the port's lighthouse, watching ships from around the world pay tribute to Heimet—the pinnacle of his reign as the merchant emperor.
