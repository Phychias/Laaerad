**Aguijio** (c. 800 BC – 710 BC) was the Regent and later Emperor of the First Heimet Dynasty, ruling the empire de facto from 760 BC until his death. He is seen as the representative figure of the empire's peak military power, yet his prolonged iron-fisted rule and extreme measures are also considered the catalyst for the dynasty's gradual decline. History often refers to him as "**The Iron-blooded Regent**" or "**The Nightmare of Rui Liandi**".

> **"As long as I live, the banner of Heimet will not fall. Even if its pole is built from a mound of bones."**

### Reign
Aguijio's reign was exceptionally long. The most pivotal events during his rule include the thorough purge of the Visyufeng family and the costly "**Rui Liandi Meat Grinder War**" launched to seize the southern gold mines.

### Biography
- **Supreme Regency and Bloody Purge** 🔥  
  After seizing power from his weak elder brother in a coup, Aguijio did not immediately proclaim himself emperor but ruled for decades under the title "Regent." During this period, he eradicated the century-old influence of the Visyufeng family from the court with extreme brutality.

- **The Rui Liandi Meat Grinder War** 💎  
  Driven by an obsession with the gold mines in the southern Rui Liandi region, Aguijio launched two large-scale southern campaigns during his reign. Although the wars ultimately secured the mines, the lengthy supply lines and guerrilla tactics of the local indigenous peoples placed immense strain on the imperial treasury. This protracted war of attrition thus earned the name "Meat Grinder War."
