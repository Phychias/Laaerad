> "I saw a perfect Tusk."

Drodauci II was the last emperor of the Heimet First Dynasty. His reign was brief, but he is remembered in history for his radical reform attempts. Although his rule ended in tragedy, he was not a foolish ruler but rather an idealist born at the wrong time. His death marked the complete end of the glorious three-hundred-year Heimet First Dynasty, ushering in the dark age of foreign rule in the land of Tusk.

## Life 📜

Drodauci II's life was full of drama and tragedy. His reign, though short, profoundly influenced the historical course of Tusk.

### Radical Reforms 🛠️
Upon ascending the throne, Drodauci II attempted to revoke the privileges his father had promised to various noble families in his later years and planned to disband private armies. These hasty reform measures instantly angered all vested interests, laying the groundwork for the subsequent coup.

### The Senate Coup ⚔️
Families such as Visyufeng, Basib, and Drodaucias quickly formed an alliance and launched a Senate coup. On a rainy night, rebel forces stormed the imperial palace. Drodauci II was horrified to discover that the guards, who had been singing his praises daily, had already defected, leaving him isolated and helpless.

### A Desolate End 🕯️
After a frantic escape, Drodauci II tried to seek support in the southern lands where his ancestors had risen to power. However, he was betrayed en route by former subordinates. He ultimately died on a muddy path leading to Crusofa, with only one loyal old servant by his side. This event not only ended his life but also symbolized the complete collapse of the Heimet First Dynasty.
