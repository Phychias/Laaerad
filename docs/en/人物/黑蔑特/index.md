The **Heimat Family** is the most tenacious and far-reaching imperial family in the history of the [Trusk Empire](../../国家与王朝/图斯克帝国/index.md).

## Origins

The family originated in the Serk Mountains. In the late [Kuileot Dynasty](../../国家与王朝/图斯克帝国/奎列奥王朝.md), they amassed astonishing wealth through the commercial trade of Hasit City, gradually infiltrating the center of power by controlling ports and through marriage alliances. The Heimet family not only laid the territorial foundation for the Trusk Empire and established its bureaucratic and provincial systems but also culturally promoted the unification and inclusivity of Sea God worship. Despite multiple instances of brutal blood feuds within the family (such as the splits during the Aguijio period and the Republican era), relying on their deep-rooted influence and political acumen, they always managed to return to the pinnacle of power through political compromise or military coups during turbulent times.

The Heimet family was also the earliest royal house in Trusk history to strengthen royal authority by controlling religious power. They utilized the existing primitive religion of Trusk, combined it with the Sea God faith transmitted from eastern colonies, and established a unified religious system. They provided theological justification for the legitimacy of the throne, ushering in a new era of centralized power in Trusk.

## Characteristics

Members of the Heimet family typically have a relatively slender build, elongated facial features, and high cheekbones. Their hair is mostly dark, and their irises exhibit two typical colors: deep brown and deep gray-blue.

The family emblem of the Heimet family is a blue-gray fish leaping above waves, symbolizing the family's origins and their consistently smooth and adaptable conduct.

## Notable Figures

| Name | Identity/Period | Brief Description and Historical Evaluation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **[Matasioksamen I](马塔肖萨门一世.md)** | Founder | **De facto founder of the dynasty**. Although he never claimed the title of Emperor, his reforms laid the foundation for family rule. He successfully repelled foreign enemies and left behind a full treasury and a well-developed tax system for posterity. |
| **[Matasioksamen II](马塔肖萨门二世.md)** | Founder of the First Dynasty | **The first Heimet Emperor**. Ascended the throne as the son-in-law of the Kuileot, completing the family's transformation from wealthy merchants to royalty. He constructed the Matasioksamen Road, symbolizing the empire's lifeline. |
| Matasioksamen III | Monarch of the First Dynasty | **Tyrant and System Establisher**. He established the brutal harem and eunuch system, ushering in the darker side of Trusk court politics. He also conquered the Zhaoxiasi territory through bloody means. |
| **[Boraspon](波拉斯蓬.md)** | Monarch of the First Dynasty | **The Empire's Most Outstanding Military Genius**. Born a bastard, he earned his place on this list through military achievements. He not only conquered Crusofa but also established the Tupero fortress, serving as the supreme executor of the empire's southern expansion strategy. |
| **[Tamog](塔莫格.md)** | Restorer of the First Dynasty | **Master of Centralization**. Reigned for over forty years, ending the prolonged interference of maternal relatives (the Visyufon family). By supporting the bureaucratic and merchant classes, he pushed imperial authority to the peak of the First Dynasty. |
| **[Aguijio](阿贵吉奥.md)** | Regent/Emperor of the First Dynasty | **Iron-fisted Dictator**. From a prince controlling the army to a de facto emperor, he launched the costly "Ruilandi Meat Grinder War". Although militarily brilliant, his harsh rule sowed the seeds for the dynasty's later turmoil. |
| **[Zhuoaxi I](卓奥西一世.md)** | Terminator of the Republic | **Political Genius and Restorer**. During the family's split and the low point of aristocratic republicanism, he unified the family branches through masterful political maneuvering, ended the rule of the seven families, and established the semi-monarchical "Lifelong Consul" system. |
| **[Zhuoaxi II](卓奥西二世.md)** | Last Monarch of the First Dynasty | **Tragic Idealist**. Attempted to forcibly centralize power in an era dominated by powerful nobles, ultimately leading to the complete collapse of the First Dynasty in a Senate coup. |
| Yingguhuo | Founder of the Second Dynasty | **Avenger After Biding His Time**. Exploited the decline of the Jaudzya Dynasty to stage a coup and re-establish the Heimet Dynasty. Through pragmatic diplomacy, including marriage alliances with barbarian tribes, he secured valuable recovery time for the empire. |
| **[Oleman](奥勒曼.md)** | Ruler of the Second Dynasty's Golden Age | **Architect of the "Silver Age"**. Under his reign, agriculture recovered, trade was monopolized, and Trusk's national power reached its zenith. He represents the Heimet family's highest capability in peacetime governance. |
| Ansette | Last Monarch of the Second Dynasty | **The Last Glimmer of a Bygone Era**. Ascended the throne amidst plague and civil war. Briefly stabilized the situation through a marriage alliance with the Tyesuku family but was ultimately betrayed and assassinated by his allies. His death marked the end of the Second Dynasty. |
| Fatuo I | Monarch of the Third Dynasty | **Short-lived Restorer**. Briefly restored the dynasty after the Tyesuku Dynasty but failed in his struggle against powerful ministers and nobles, dying at the "Sunset Banquet". |
