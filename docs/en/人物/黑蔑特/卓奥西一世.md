> "The Last Philosopher-King"

**Drodauci** (c. 640 BCE – 550 BCE) was a decisive political and military figure in the late period of the "aristocratic republic" following the collapse of the First Dynasty. Renowned for his masterful political maneuvering and ruthless decision-making, he possessed immense personal political and military prestige. Ultimately, under the title of "Lifetime Consul," he ended the rule of the seven great families, paving the way for subsequent imperial centralization.

Drodauci is generally regarded as a pragmatic politician. During the decades-long internecine warfare among the families, he did not rely on initial overwhelming strength. Instead, through meticulous alliances, sowing discord, and strategic patience, he eventually enabled his own branch of the Heimet family to seize supreme power.

### 📜 Biography
* **Ending the Rule of Seven Families**: In the later period of the "rule of seven families," Drodauci maneuvered among the various families. Overtly, he supported the Visyufeng family, while covertly funding the commercial activities of the Basib family, thereby exacerbating and exploiting the conflicts between them. When the main rivals were exhausted from internal strife, he led the Heimet family in a decisive strike, ending the period of shared rule.
* **Purges and Exile**: To consolidate absolute control over the Heimet family, Drodauci carried out a ruthless internal purge. He exiled the unyielding third branch to the wild southern borderlands of the empire.
* **Establishing the Ruling Framework**: Despite wielding absolute power surpassing that of previous monarchs, Drodauci, to placate the old aristocracy, never formally declared himself emperor. Instead, he adopted the title of "Lifetime Consul." With a pragmatic spirit, he redrew the empire's borders, voluntarily relinquishing remote enclaves that were difficult to control effectively. He concentrated ruling resources on the core regions, allowing the teetering state to survive.

### 🏛️ Legacy of Rule
Drodauci's reign marked the transition from an aristocratic republic to personal centralized rule. The "Lifetime Consul" system he established, though lacking the name of emperor, functioned as one in practice. The core territory he defined and his model of centralization were directly adopted by his successors and eventually evolved into the complete imperial system.
