# <ruby>Tyesuku<rt>Tyesuku</rt></ruby>

**Tyesuku** (Truskean: _tyesuku_) is a pivotal concept from the early imperial period of Laaerad. It refers both to the prominent family that ruled western Trusk—the **Tyesuku family**—and to the geographical name of their hereditary domain and the later-established **Tyesuku Minor Kingdom**.

## Etymology and Meaning

Linguists trace the term **Tyesuku** back to [Proto-Laaeradian](../../语言/拉埃拉德原始语.md).

-   **Root Composition**: Derived from _tyesu_ (warm, hot) and _ku_ (stone).
-   **Original Meaning**: "Warm stone" or "hot stone." Scholars widely agree that this term initially served as a vivid description of the prevalent **blacksmithing** profession in the area—symbolizing the process of repeatedly heating and forging iron ore in a furnace.
-   **Semantic Evolution**: As a local family, originally akin to a blacksmiths' guild, gradually gained political power, the term evolved from a description of a profession/local product into a family surname and subsequently a place name.

## The Tyesuku Family

The Tyesuku family is a top-tier aristocratic lineage that runs throughout Trusk history. Its family history is a microcosm of the power transitions within the Trusk Empire.

### Early Period: Latency and Rise

Before the emergence of **Tyesuku Webo**, historical inscriptions contain only sporadic and marginal records of the family, mostly depicting them as minor local officials reporting to the capital. It wasn't until **Tyesuku Webo** became the family head that the family formally stepped onto the historical stage. During the period of the [Second Heimet Dynasty](黑蔑特王朝.md#黑蔑特第二王朝), Webo utilized the internal turmoil of the dynasty to gradually accumulate political capital.

### [The Tyesuku Dynasty](../../国家与王朝/图斯克帝国/铁苏库王朝.md)

The family's first peak of power began with **Dezhuohei I**.

The third emperor of the Second Heimet Dynasty (the last king, Ansaite) had no direct male heirs. As the emperor's nephew (some sources say son-in-law and chancellor), Dezhuohei I pacified the realm in the chaotic period following a plague and ultimately took over the government, initiating the **First Tyesuku Dynasty**.

### Mid-Period: Latency During the Botessizhuahu Period

The First Dynasty did not last long, and the empire subsequently fell under the rule of the **Botessizhuahu Dynasty**. Although the direct descendants of Dezhuohei I were integrated into the Botessizhuahu imperial family through marriage, the collateral branch of the Tyesuku family—the line of **Webo II**—did not die out. They were enfeoffed back to their ancestral land, **Tyesuku City**, where they bided their time and accumulated strength.

### [The Second Tyesuku Dynasty](铁苏库王朝.md#铁苏库第二王朝)

Years later, **Dezhuohei III**, as the family leader, led the uprising that overthrew the Botessizhuahu Dynasty. He successfully ascended the imperial throne once again, establishing the **Second Tyesuku Dynasty**. He established the Trusk Empire's first truly centralized rule and is regarded by later generations as the empire's true imperial ruler.

## The Tyesuku Region (Tyesuku Minor Kingdom)

The **Tyesuku Region**, administratively known as the **Tyesuku Minor Kingdom**.

### Historical Development

-   **Origin**: Initially referred only to **Tyesuku City** at the end of the Kelusuofa River in the southern part of the [Trusk Basin](../../地理与区域/图斯克/index.md) and its surroundings. This was the earliest fiefdom granted to the Tyesuku family during the [Second Heimet Dynasty](黑蔑特王朝.md#黑蔑特第二王朝).
-   **Expansion**: Through Dezhuohei I's military campaigns and the later establishment of the Second Tyesuku Dynasty, the region was consolidated into a major kingdom, hereditarily governed by a prince of the Tyesuku family (the King of Tyesuku).

### Geographical Territory

The kingdom is located in the western part of the empire and primarily consists of the following regions:

-   **Tyesuku Mountains**: The natural barrier to the west.
-   **Genara Highlands**: A strategic highland connecting to the south.
-   **Tyesuku Royal Domain**: The core ruling area.
-   **Andon Lands**: An important agricultural and population area.
-   **Pernan Lands**: Located in the upper reaches of the Pernan River.

### Political System

Unlike the commandery-county system in the empire's core regions, the Tyesuku Kingdom adopted a **feudal system**. Lands were primarily enfeoffed to generals who had performed outstanding military service in the unification wars and to the old retainers of the Tyesuku family (such as the collateral branches of the Webo line), forming a military aristocracy that guarded the empire's western flank.
