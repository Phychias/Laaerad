## Overview

<ruby><b>Decoheim</b><rt>Decoheim</rt></ruby> I was a pivotal figure in the history of the [Trusk Empire](../../国家与王朝/图斯克帝国/index.md) and the founder of the [Tyesuku Dynasty](../../国家与王朝/图斯克帝国/铁苏库王朝.md). During his reign, he established the initial framework for many of the empire's future institutions and fostered the nascent concept of an imperial citizenship that transcended ethnic boundaries. Although his rule was marked by turbulence and controversy, he undoubtedly laid a solid foundation for the long-lasting Tyesuku Dynasty that followed, earning him profound reverence and commemoration in the ancestral temple by later emperors of the dynasty.

## 🧒 Early Life

### Birth and Naming
Decoheim was born on a crisp autumn day when the sky displayed the rare phenomenon of "two suns sharing the sky." Even in the well-traveled imperial capital of [[Hasit City]], this sight was extraordinary. His father, Duke Weber Tyesuku, named him "Decoheim," meaning "Divided Day," based on ancient records.

### Speech Impediment and Temple Life
Decoheim remained unable to speak until the age of three, only sitting quietly in a daze, which worried his family. After seeking medical help in vain, they turned to the local God Gyua *Tanka* (temple). After meditating and practicing with the temple's *Jiaohuzayi* (a type of monk), he began to utter sporadic syllables. From then on, Decoheim regularly visited the temple, either listening to myths and rituals or sitting in silent meditation with the Jiaohuzayi.

Around the age of six, he suddenly began to speak, but the language was incomprehensible to anyone. It was neither Truskean nor Laprio, and even the Jiaohuzayi who understood [[Glömezher]] could not identify it. About a year later, he taught himself to write in ancient Glömezher script. It wasn't until the eve of his seventh birthday, when boiling water was left unattended at home, that he urgently shouted "Be careful!" in Glömezher, which was finally understood by his family. Thereafter, he gradually became able to communicate in Glömezher. Due to his unique way of speaking, he was privately called "*Little Jiaohuzayi*" by the servants.

### Education and Growth
After regaining his language ability, Decoheim was sent to another Tanka near the imperial palace to study. He quickly mastered written Truskean, but his spoken language retained a peculiar word order. Following a period of quiet study, he once became passionate about adventure, attempting to cross the Heimet Strait with friends. However, due to storms resulting in one death and one injury among his companions, he was confined to home by his father.

At the age of ten, his father Weber sent him to serve as a page in the imperial guard, a common practice for socializing and training noble children. He was assigned to the side palace of the Third Prince, Ansait. Due to his earlier reputation as the "Little *Jiaohuzayi*," Ansait, who held expectations for Lingyu (a form of "magic" dependent on talent), paid him special attention, and the two formed a friendship. In the palace, Decoheim not only trained his physique and gained military insight but also, under the guidance of the court *Jiaohuzayi* Ludimart, extensively read Lingyu texts in the scripture repository, glimpsing the "realms" of Lingyu. During this time, he also frequently interacted with the Second Prince Hetuke, who was highly skilled in Lingyu.

## ⚔️ Imperial Turmoil and Rise

### Plague and Conscription
A plague sweeping the empire changed the course of Decoheim's life. The plague claimed the lives of the emperor and his mother, and the southern Laprio people seized the opportunity to rebel. Decoheim was conscripted by the imperial navy, serving as the captain's adjutant on the patrol vessel *Andonmart*, whose captain was Ludimart, a Jaudzyan from a military family.

### Encounter and Hardship
During his naval service, Decoheim encountered the Laprio girl **Shegvi**, and through a series of events and fighting side by side against enemies, they developed mutual affection. Shegvi lost an eye protecting him. Decoheim brought her back to the ship for treatment, which caused discontent among the crew. Ultimately, he had to escort her ashore to find her scattered tribe. Although he briefly joined her tribe, before their agreed reunion, Decoheim was attacked by assassins, separated from Shegvi, and forced to return to the ship.

### Crisis and Turning Point on the Ship
The plague spread to the ship. Decoheim, while ill, claimed to have received a revelation from the God Gyua. After recovering, he surprisingly demonstrated the ability to "cure" this plague, greatly increasing his prestige. Subsequently, internal strife erupted on the ship due to uncertainty about the future. Although Decoheim refused to take sides, the conflict led to crew members killing each other while the plague raged, drastically reducing their numbers. Finally, under the guidance of the recovered Captain Ludimart, Decoheim began learning leadership, stabilized the situation, and led the ship back to the besieged Heimet Port after battles.

### Imperial Capital Conspiracy and Decision
Decoheim infiltrated the besieged [Hasit City](../../地理/图斯克/黑蔑特/哈希特城.md) with close associates to search for his aunt but failed. However, he infiltrated the imperial palace and discovered the Third Prince Ansait imprisoned by the Second Prince Hetuke. Ansait informed him that the late emperor's will had originally passed the throne to him, and it was Hetuke who had usurped power. Decoheim rescued Ansait and decided to assist him in traveling to the [Trusk Basin](../../地理/图斯克/index.md) to seek reinforcements, while also searching for his father and aunt along the way.

### Southern Campaign and Discovery
En route, Decoheim recruited his father's former guard captain Presp and his men, learning that his father had died suppressing the rebellion in the south, and that his aunt Wisera had been separated in the chaos but might have survived. With his forces growing, he decided to capture the **Greenland Fortress**, a key point on the route to the basin. The fortress was occupied by Laprio forces but lightly defended. Decoheim, for the first time in a leadership role, gave a pre-battle mobilization speech and successfully captured the city in a night assault.

In the fortress's underground, he discovered a **Teleportation Array**, realizing this was the core application of Laprio Lingyu and the key that allowed their ancestors to establish the Novaya Dynasty. Utilizing this network of arrays, Decoheim rapidly deployed troops and supplies, successively recapturing cities in the Krusofa region, and finally conquering his family's ancestral home, [Tyesuku City](../../../zh/人物/铁苏库/index.md).

## 🏰 Early Reign

### Enfeoffment as King
In Tyesuku City, Decoheim met his half-brother Weber II, confirming his father's death. The following spring, during the campaign, he withdrew his troops due to a Lingyu summons and miraculously found his long-lost aunt Wisera in a remote village, deepening his belief in Lingyu thereafter.

Once the situation stabilized somewhat, Ansait formally ascended the throne in Tyesuku, becoming **Heimet Ansait III**, and enfeoffed Decoheim as **King of Tyesuku**, granting him Krusofa and the surrounding territories. That year, Decoheim was twenty-three years old.

### Pacifying the South
In the following years, using Krusofa as his base and leveraging the continuously discovered teleportation array network for efficient deployment and mobility, Decoheim continued to sweep through strongholds occupied by Laprio forces in the south. He successfully reintegrated the Laprio region into imperial control, laying the groundwork for his later, more extensive reforms and rule.
