> The ruler is trust.

Nortesot is a classic example in the history of the [Heimet First Dynasty](../../国家与王朝/图斯克帝国/黑蔑特王朝.md#黑蔑特第一王朝) of a noble acting as a maternal relative of the royal family interfering in state affairs.

## Biography

Nortesot took the concept of a "powerful minister" to its extreme. He was cold, shrewd, and utterly unburdened by morality. His political acumen was precise, and his calculations far-reaching, unmatched by anyone of his time. When Prince Yepken staged a coup, deposing the legitimate emperor Mapetumat and installing his young nephew Ebmakt as a puppet, most of the old ministers chose to submit or go into exile. However, Nortesot saw opportunity within the crisis. He lay in wait, using the intricate network he had meticulously woven over the past two decades within the Imperial Guard and palace eunuchs, gradually setting the stage for his counterattack.

His actions were calm and brutal. Even after Ebmakt's formal coronation, which granted him a veneer of legitimacy, Nortesot remained undeterred. He orchestrated a seemingly ordinary royal hunt, but arranged an "accidental" incident in the dense forest, causing the young emperor Ebmakt to fall from his horse and die. Subsequently, he swiftly seized control of the palace and, by force, reinstated the deposed and imprisoned emperor Mapetumat to the throne, while he himself assumed supreme authority under the title "Imperial Regent and Chancellor." This series of maneuvers not only eliminated the puppet emperor but also completely sidelined the influence of Prince Yepken, causing a drastic shift of power into his own hands.

After consolidating power, Nortesot further strengthened military control. He privately redeployed the elite "Turha Army," originally tasked with guarding the capital, to be permanently stationed at the Peturo Fortress line, engaging in a military standoff lasting over a decade with the remnants of Yepken's forces, known as the "Prince's Faction." During this period, he repeatedly issued conscription orders and tax increase decrees in the emperor's name, but in reality, all resources were used to consolidate his personal power. Emperor Mapetumat was completely reduced to a symbolic tool in his hands, his only remaining function being to affix the imperial seal to edicts—and even the seal was kept by Nortesot's trusted aides.

During the decades of his de facto rule over the empire, the Trusk Empire appeared to be under the reign of Mapetumat, but in essence, it was under the military dictatorship of the Visyufon family. Nortesot established a bureaucratic and military system centered on personal loyalty, with all key positions held by his family members or trusted associates. Economically, he monopolized the eastern trade routes and mining operations; judicially, he often bypassed the law to deal with political enemies directly under the pretext of "military necessity." Although later historical texts often label him as the "Kingslayer," during his lifetime, no one in the entire empire dared to openly challenge his authority. With his ruthless yet highly efficient rule, Nortesot proved that in times of chaos, naked power often holds more sway than legitimate titles.
