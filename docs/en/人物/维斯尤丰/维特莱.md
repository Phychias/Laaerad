> *Family Above All*

## Biography

Wetlai was indeed the gravedigger of the [Heimet First Dynasty](../../国家与王朝/图斯克帝国/黑蔑特王朝.md#黑蔑特第一王朝), but he was not born a traitor. As the representative of the old aristocratic faction at court, everything he did was essentially to protect the privileges and vested interests accumulated by the Visyufeng family over centuries. When the young and idealistic Emperor Zhuoaoxi II, after assuming personal rule, issued the *Decree for Reducing Feudal Powers* in an attempt to weaken local lords and strengthen central authority, Wetlai sensed an unprecedented crisis. In his view, this was not merely a suppression of local powers but a shake-up of the very foundation upon which the traditional aristocracy survived.

On a night of torrential rain, Wetlai personally rode to the West Gate of Hasit City. Using the Governor's warrant, he tricked the guards into opening the gates and secretly facilitated the entry of the allied forces of the Zhaoxiasi and Basib families. At the outset of planning this coup, his intention was not to completely overthrow the Heimet Dynasty. Rather, he merely wanted to replace the emperor with a more "compliant" one who would respect the interests of the old aristocracy—perhaps by installing a young member of the imperial collateral line, with himself holding real power as regent. However, he gravely underestimated the uncontrollable chain reaction once a coup was set in motion.

With the "accidental" death of Emperor Zhuoaoxi II during his exile, the direct imperial lineage was completely severed. Local lords everywhere seized the opportunity to declare independence. The new regime that Wetlai had believed he could control never truly materialized, and the dynasty collapsed within a few short months. More ironically, the Visyufeng family also lost the host upon which it depended for survival—they were parasitic nobles attached to the dynastic system. Once the dynasty disintegrated, lacking both popular support and military strength, they were ultimately swept away in the tide of northern nomadic cavalry sweeping southwards. Centuries of glory vanished into thin air. The choice Wetlai made, starting from the desire to protect his family, instead became the prelude to its utter destruction.
