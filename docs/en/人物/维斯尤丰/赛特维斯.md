# Seitwise

Seitwise was the founder of the Visyufeng family's power structure and a pivotal figure in the transition of the Hasit Empire from decline to prosperity. During the late reign of Borasapon II, the empire's finances were increasingly depleted due to years of warfare and poor governance. Particularly after the loss of the Ruilian gold mines—a lifeline that once accounted for thirty percent of the empire's annual revenue—which fell into the hands of northern tribes, the imperial family not only saw a sharp decline in revenue but also a complete loss of prestige. It was amidst such turmoil that Seitwise, leveraging the substantial wealth and connections accumulated by her family over generations, supported the crumbling court in her capacity as empress. She secretly replenished the depleted treasury, maintained the salaries of the imperial guard, and funded a series of projects to rebuild temples and public baths. On the surface, this preserved the empire's former dignity, allowing the Hasit court to maintain its awe-inspiring ceremonial grandeur even during the crisis.

## Biography

When Borasapon II passed away, there were no surviving direct descendants in the imperial line, and various regional families and military commanders were eyeing the throne. At this critical juncture, Seitwise demonstrated her extraordinary political acumen and decisiveness. She did not choose to align with any existing faction. Instead, she overcame strong opposition—particularly from the western warlords who championed the traditional "father-to-son succession"—and boldly broke with the old conventions of imperial succession. After months of secret negotiations and resource exchanges, she persuaded the Senate and the priesthood to place her eight-year-old son (some court records suggest he was actually her nephew, adopted from her sister) Grusabot on the throne, making him the youngest monarch in Hasit history.

Seizing this opportunity, Seitwise did not stop at regency. Under the pretext of "assisting the young sovereign and rectifying the inner court," she established the unprecedented position of "Chamberlain of the Inner Court." This role not only oversaw court personnel, finances, and confidential documents but, crucially, its appointment was monopolized by the Visyufeng family. The first Chamberlain was her own brother, Kalion. This move effectively institutionalized the transformation of her family's influence from that of a regional power into a pillar entrenched at the very heart of imperial authority. Through the Chamberlain's network, members of the Visyufeng family gradually took control of critical functions such as tax auditing, official supervision, and border intelligence. The once-decentralized imperial power quietly coalesced under the family's control, laying an unshakable foundation for the subsequent century-long "Visyufeng Era."
