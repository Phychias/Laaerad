# A Study of Common Names in the Trusk Region

The naming traditions in the Trusk region are closely related to its complex linguistic history, geographical distribution, and social strata. From the coastal core of the Empire to the Glömezher tribes in the western highlands, and further to the ancient Laprio city-states along the Novaya Sea, distinct cultural spheres exhibit marked differences in their naming systems. This article aims to organize these common names across regions and explain their etymology and cultural connotations.

## 1 Trusk Empire

As the dominant civilization on the continent, the naming system of the Strait Truskean language (truskil mielail) features a rigorous class structure and analytical characteristics. Nobility and elites often adopt structurally complex compound words to highlight family history or moral pursuits; commoner names are more descriptive, frequently related to natural scenery or birthplace.

### 1.1 Noble Surnames (naamar)

Trusk noble surnames often retain archaic compound structures or traces of the original Laaerad language. In terms of word formation, they frequently employ object-verb (OV) or modifier-first structures, which, within the predominantly SVO Strait Truskean language, are considered solemn and conceptualized expressions.

**Royalty and Military Nobility**
The most prominent surnames are often related to rulership, war, and protection.

- **tyesuku**: Derived from `tyesu` (warm) and `ku` (stone), meaning "warm stone," possibly indicating the family's early involvement in blacksmithing.
- **moswarike**: Meaning "rider" (_moswari_ ride + _ke_ practitioner), belonging to the earliest Glömezher tribe to settle in Trusk.
- **turhaike**: Meaning "warrior" (_turhai_ fight + _ke_ person), symbolizing martial spirit, an important military merit family, and also one of the Truskeanized branches of the Moswarike family.
- **jaujia**: Meaning "glorious guardian" (_jaujia_ guard).
- **kuinsyec**: Meaning "golden sword" or "king's sword," inherited from the Kuileot dynasty era.
- **laigrakou**: Meaning "great lord" or "leader" (_gai_ great + _grakou_ lord/master), originating from tribal-era chieftain titles.

**Religious Authority and Ancient Lineage**
Some surnames originate from creation myths or nature worship, symbolizing the family's ancient sacrificial rights or spiritual abilities.

- **heimarit**: Meaning "sun-given" or "heaven's grace" (_heim_ sun + _arit_ give), legend says their ancestors were divinely appointed.
- **tauswi**: Meaning "sky spirit" (_tau_ sky + _swi_ spirit), an ancient surname with shamanistic or spiritualistic connotations.
- **hambwasia**: Meaning "myth" or "epic," typically belonging to historian families.
- **hetuke**: Meaning "nightwalker" (_hetu_ night + _ke_ person), possibly related to God Gyua faith or night watch duties.
- **numsib**: Meaning "thousand leaves" (_numrea_ thousand + _sib_ leaf), originating from the religious concept of the "翻海宗" (Fanhaezong), symbolizing prosperity and change.

**Administration and Wealth**
With the development of the Empire's commercial and bureaucratic systems, a group of surnames emerged reflecting wealth, territory, and administrative functions.

- **ruilansom**: Meaning "wealthy person" (_ruilan_ property + _som_ adjectivizer), one of the early pioneering families around Heimet city, later granted mining rights and renamed the area ruilian, from which the family derived this surname.
- **goiraudun**: Meaning "grand hall" (_goirau_ hall + _dun_ augmentative), belonging to an ancient artisan family.
- **nowdun**: Meaning "vast sea," common among minor nobility in port cities along the Novaya Sea coast.
- **eratuku**: Meaning "landowner" (_erat_ land + _uku_ master), originating from the position of colonial officials dispatched by the Empire, later evolving into a surname.
- **wisuku**: Meaning "forest owner," similar to Eratuku, but territories are mostly forested.
- **guduke**: Meaning "mender" or "storer" (_gudu_ sew/mend + _ke_ person), originating from artisans or warehouse officials.

**Virtues and Qualities**
Many nobles adopted surnames based on abstract virtues or aspirations, highlighting the family's moral standing.

- **lousan**: Meaning "righteous path" (_lous_ path + _an_ correct), said to have been adopted by the family that designed the Matasioksamen road.
- **porlasom**: Meaning "ancient and upright" or "traditionalist" (_por_ old + _las_ straight), symbolizing integrity and tradition.
- **ilesait**: Meaning "brilliant," an ancient family known for producing many priestesses.
- **giateis**: Meaning "secret knowledge" (_gia_ hidden + _teis_ knowledge), symbolizing mastery of esoteric knowledge.
- **norkosom**: Meaning "prolific," expressing the wish for family prosperity.
- **anioksait**: Meaning "thorough" or "cycle" (_aniok_ turn), implying completeness.
- **ludimart**: Meaning "land of joy" (_ludi_ play + _mart_ place), symbolizing pleasure and harmony.
- **drodlaisom**: Meaning "thoughtful" (_drod_ heart/mind + _lais_ long + _som_ adjectivizer), symbolizing a steady character.
- **aritsom**: Symbolizing generosity and keeping promises.
- **aseasait**: Meaning "extraordinary," symbolizing unique qualities.
- **lasjodrod**: Meaning "broad-hearted" (_laisiok_ wide + _drod_ heart), symbolizing tolerance and magnanimity.

**Nature Totems**
Some families use natural objects as surnames, reflecting a connection with the environment.

- **seitwise**: Meaning "fruit tree" (_seit_ fruit + _wise_ tree), symbolizing vitality.
- **basib**: Meaning "water plant" (_ba_ water + _sib_ leaf), common among families from watery regions.
- **barol**: Meaning "deep water" (_ba_ water + _rol_ deep), symbolizing tranquility.
- **laiswise**: Meaning "tall tree" (_lais_ long/tall + _wise_ tree), symbolizing uprightness.
- **samemgreke**: Meaning "mountain people" or "mountain keeper," symbolizing resilience.
- **betusait**: Meaning "cold" (_betu_ cold), often originating from northeastern immigrants.

### 1.2 Noble Given Names (namo)

Noble given names place greater emphasis on personal qualities and aspirations, often derived from verbs or combinations of nouns and adjectives.

**Imperial and Leadership Qualities**

- **laiswi**: Meaning "great spirit" (_lai_ great + _swi_ spirit), symbolizing immense spiritual power.
- **teiskwa**: Meaning "the known one" (_teis_ know + _kwa_ passive), expressing the wish for fame throughout the land.
- **yingukho**: Meaning "certain victory" (_ying_ must + _gukho_ win), expressing belief in victory.
- **oleman**: Meaning "good fruit" (_olem_ fruit/result + _an_ good), implying a fulfilling life.

**Martial Spirit and Action**

- **kansoi**: Meaning "swift sword" (_kan_ sword + _soi_ swift), hoping for exceptional swordsmanship.
- **jeposoi**: Meaning "swift walker" (_jepo_ walk + _soi_ swift), symbolizing swift action.
- **jaujyaan**: Meaning "loyal guardian" (_jaujya_ guard + _an_ good), aspiring to be an excellent protector.
- **guit**: Meaning "stone user," originating from ancient slingers.
- **gudun**: Meaning "great stone" (_gu_ stone + _dun_ augmentative), symbolizing steadfastness.
- **wetlai**: Meaning "strong wind," symbolizing decisiveness and force.
- **wetpo**: Meaning "wind-like," symbolizing lightness and swiftness.

**Wisdom, Spirit, and Virtue**

- **drodaucy**: Meaning "calm heart" (_drod_ heart + _aucy_ calm), implying composure.
- **ningswi**: Meaning "spiritual power," symbolizing spiritual potential.
- **tyaan**: Meaning "good will" (_tyaa_ will + _an_ good), symbolizing good intentions.
- **azozi**: Meaning "fearless" (_a-_ without + _zozi_ fear), a negative derivation name, expressing hope for bravery.
- **porteis**: Meaning "prophet," common among scholars or priests.
- **porteiske**: Meaning "great shaman," referring to one who can make prophecies come true.
- **soryaz**: Meaning "weaver" (_sori_ weave + _yaz_ state), symbolizing creativity and order-building.
- **troleug**: Meaning "flint person" or "fire-maker," symbolizing wisdom and innovation.
- **dayaud**: Meaning "redemption" (_de-_ causative + _a-ud_ not-die), a recursive derivation, often used for healers.
- **ansait**: Meaning "well-being," expressing simple blessings.

**Nature and Beauty**

- **wespan**: Meaning "exceptional beauty" (_wespa_ color/beauty + _an_ beautiful), describing outstanding appearance.
- **sniowan**: Meaning "pleasing to the eye," describing handsome/beautiful looks.
- **heimbro**: Meaning "rising sun" (_heim_ sun + _bor_ start), symbolizing new life and hope.
- **rigujiu**: Meaning "white snow," symbolizing noble character.
- **bafara**: Meaning "flowing water," symbolizing a smooth life.
- **baik**: Meaning "water-like," describing a gentle temperament.
- **weten**: Meaning "wind-following," symbolizing freedom and ease.

## 2 Glömezher Tribes

The Glömezher language is agglutinative, and its naming style is rugged, often related to fierce beasts and natural forces.

**Clan Names**

- **khenxed**: Meaning "black wolf," known for fierceness and love of battle.
- **olaleuk**: Meaning "fire head," a tribe that worships the fire god.
- **mörikesep**: Meaning "tall horse," skilled in horseback archery, a branch formed from the Moswarike family through phonetic changes.
- **rigurchi**: Meaning "snow clan," inhabiting the far northern ice fields.

**Personal Names**

- **vesnö-sar**: Meaning "hunter," an occupational expert name.
- **bordas-kim**: Meaning "upright person."
- **gok-ola**: Meaning "iron head" or "sky head."
- **joluk-xar**: Meaning "the furious one."
- **ila-sora**: Meaning "brilliant."
- **taamek**: Meaning "sky guest," also used as a greeting meaning "hello."

## 3 Ancient Laprio City-States

The ancient Laprio language, influenced by maritime trade, features fluent diphthongs and inflectional characteristics, and developed a gender article system (Le/La) based on the concept of "mundane/natural."

**Family/Guild Names**
Typically begin with **La** (distant/natural gender), indicating a long family history.

- **La Valau**: Meaning "sea family," shipping magnates.
- **La Kuoju**: Meaning "silver family," bankers or silver mine owners.
- **La Hura**: Meaning "time family," ancient clockmakers or prophets.
- **La Sedia**: Meaning "forest family," timber merchants.

**Personal Names**
Males or secular professions often use **Le** class (proximate gender), while females or religious roles often use **La** class (distant gender).

- **Arino**: Le-class name, meaning "merchant."
- **Lauro**: Le-class name, meaning "majestic one."
- **Foko**: Le-class name, meaning "flyer," often referring to messengers.
- **Vala**: La-class name, meaning "sea woman."
- **Maura**: La-class name, meaning "cloud" or "mist."
- **Leuga**: La-class name, meaning "fierce fire," often referring to passionate or red-haired women.
