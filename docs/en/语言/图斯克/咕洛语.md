<b><ruby>GlГ¶mezher<rt>GlГ¶mer</rt></ruby></b> was the lingua franca of the nomadic peoples in the northwestern region of Laaerad during the Middle Ages. With the rise of the GlГ¶mezher Dynasty, this language evolved from a tribal dialect into the official language of the steppe regime. Although GlГ¶mezher speakers were historically often regarded as barbarians by the southern sedentary peoples, the language itself possesses a rigorous and elegant vowel harmony system and a complex case system.

This book is a detailed grammatical description of GlГ¶mezher, compiled based on comparative studies of surviving inscriptions from the nomadic period and existing oral dialects. GlГ¶mezher is a typical agglutinative language. Its phonological features differ significantly from Truskean in the eastern part of the continent, with the most prominent characteristic being a rich vowel inventory and strict vowel harmony rules.

## 1 Phonology

The **phonology** of GlГ¶mezher exhibits clear vowel harmony.

The standard GlГ¶mezher **vowel** system consists of six core phonemes (a, e, o, Г¶, u, Гј) and one neutral vowel (i). Vowel aperture and tongue position play a decisive role in morphological changes.

The standard GlГ¶mezher **consonant** inventory is relatively rich, distinguishing voicing and aspiration, and having contrasts at the velar and uvular places of articulation.

### 1.2 Phonotactics and Prosody

GlГ¶mezher is a highly agglutinative language. The attachment of affixes follows strict phonotactic rules, the most core of which is **vowel harmony**.

#### 1.2.1 Vowel Harmony

This is the cornerstone of GlГ¶mezher word formation. The vowel of a suffix must agree with the root vowel in terms of the "front/back" property.

1. **Basic Rule**:
    
    - **Front vowel root** (e, Г¶, Гј) $\to$ takes a **front vowel suffix**.
        
    - **Back vowel root** (a, o, u) $\to$ takes a **back vowel suffix**.
        
2. **Neutral Vowel Rule**:
    
    - **i** is considered a neutral (or transparent) vowel. Suffix choice is **not** influenced by `i` in the root; instead, it follows the vowel preceding the `i`.
        
    - If the root contains only `i`, it is usually treated by convention (mostly as front vowel).
        
3. **Long Vowels**:
    
    - Long vowels (e.g., `aa`, `ii`) have the same harmony property as their short vowel counterparts.
        

**Harmony Examples Table:**

| ------------- | ----------------------- | --------------------------------- | ------------------- |
| **Front Vowel** | `vir` (to listen - treated as front) | -ge                               | `virge` (is listening) |
| **Back Vowel** | `jyom` (to see)         | -ggu                              | `jyomggu` (is seeing) |

#### 1.2.2 Consonant Clusters and Assimilation

At morpheme boundaries, consonants undergo significant assimilation:

1. **Voicing Assimilation**:
    
    - **Voicing**: voiceless + voiced $\to$ voiced + voiced (e.g., `/pd/` $\to$ `/bd/`, spelling unchanged).
        
    - **Devoicing**: voiced + voiceless $\to$ voiceless + voiceless (less common, often word-finally).
        
2. **Nasal Assimilation**:
    
    - nasal + stop $\to$ homorganic nasal + stop (e.g., `/np/` $\to$ `/mp/`, spelled as `mb` or `mp`).
        
3. **Liquid Simplification**:
    
    - `/l/` or `/КЂ/` clusters simplify to the first liquid (e.g., `/lКЂ/` $\to$ `/l/`).
        
4. **Special Sound Changes**:
    
    - **x/r Merger**: `/ЙЈ/` + `/КЂ/` $\to$ `/КЂ/` (spelled as `r`). In southern dialects, word-initial `x` is often pronounced as `r`.
        
    - **Stop Lenition**: `/p, t, k/` may lenite to voiced sounds or even elide in unstressed syllables or word-finally.

### 1.3 Stress

GlГ¶mezher stress rules are relatively fixed:

- **Polysyllabic words**: Stress tends to fall on the **penultimate syllable**, e.g., `vala'sali`.
    
- **Disyllabic words**: Stress tends to fall on the **first syllable**, e.g., `'hiКЂa`.

### 1.5 Note: Dialectal Differences in Orthography

When reading ancient texts or texts from different regions, note the following orthographic and pronunciation correspondences across dialects:

- **x/r interchange**: Due to the perceptual similarity between the phonemes `/ЙЈ/` and `/КЂ/`, some dialects interchange them, e.g., `sexer` (here) pronounced as `serer`.

## 2 Morphology

### 2.1 Verbs

GlГ¶mezher verbs have extremely rich morphological changes and are the core of sentence syntactic structure. As an agglutinative language, its verb construction follows a strict **slot** system. A complete verb phrase often carries various grammatical categories such as voice, negation, aspect, tense, and mood.

#### 2.1.1 Morphological Structure Overview

The construction formula for a GlГ¶mezher verb is as follows:

$$\mathbf{Prefix} \text{ (negation/voice)} + \mathbf{ROOT} \text{ (root)} + \mathbf{Aspect} \text{ (aspect)} + \mathbf{Tense/Mood} \text{ (tense/mood)}$$

Explanation of each level:

1. **Prefix Zone**: Responsible for changing the verb's valency (e.g., passive, causative) or logical property (negation).
    
2. **Root**: Carries the core semantics.
    
3. **Suffix Zone (Inner)**: Marks the state of the action (aspect), most closely attached to the root.
    
4. **Suffix Zone (Outer)**: Marks the temporal location of the action (tense) or the speaker's attitude (mood/modality).
