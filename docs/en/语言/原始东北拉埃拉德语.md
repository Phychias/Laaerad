# Proto-Northeast Laeladic (PNEL)

!!! Abstract "Preface"
	Proto-Northeast Laeladic (abbreviated as PNEL) is the common ancestor of Truskean (ST) and Glömezher (GL).
	Based on the latest comparative analysis of the ST lexicon and GL, we have established the core phonological features of PNEL:
	1.  **Vowel length contrast**: This is the source of phonological divergence in the two descendant languages (ST diphthongization vs. GL vowel harmony).
	2.  **Three-way stop contrast**: Voiced, voiceless unaspirated, and voiceless aspirated.
	3.  **Uvular series**: Traces remain in GL but are completely lost in ST.
	4.  **Penultimate stress**: GL retains this rule (thus preserving rich word endings), while ST underwent **stress shift to the left** (leading to apocope and a trend towards analytic morphology).

---

## 1 Phonology

### 1.1 Vowel System

We reconstruct PNEL as having **6 core vowels**, each with a **length contrast** (_/V/ vs /Vː/_).

|       | **Front** | **Central** | **Back** |
| ----- | ------- | ------- | ------- |
| **High** | *i, *iː | *ɨ, *ɨː | *u, *uː |
| **Mid** | *e, *eː |         | *o, *oː |
| **Low** |         | *a, *aː |         |

#### 1.1.1 Vowel Evolution Mechanisms

- **PNEL `*ɨ` (Central High Vowel)**:
    
    - **ST**: Typically merges into `i` or `u` (depending on surrounding consonants).
        
    - **GL**: Merges into `i` (neutral vowel).
        
- **Fate of Long Vowels (`*Vː`)**:
    
    - **ST (Diphthongization)**: Long vowels became unstable and broke into **diphthongs**. This is the direct reason for ST's rich diphthongs (_ai, au, oi, ou_).
        
        - _Example_: PNEL `*laː` (big) $\to$ ST `lai`.
            
    - **GL (Harmony)**: Long vowels shortened but triggered vowel harmony mechanisms.
        
        - _Example_: PNEL `*laː` (big) $\to$ GL `laa` (orthographically doubled, pronounced long or half-long, retaining back vowel properties).

#### 1.2.1 Stop Chain Shift Rules

Based on comparisons between the ST lexicon (e.g., _badaa_ four) and GL (_pata_ four), we confirm the hypothesized chain shift phenomenon:

1. **PNEL Voiced Stops (`*b, *d, *g`)**:
    
    - **ST**: **Devoiced to unaspirated stops**.
        
        - _Example_: PNEL `*badaː` (four) $\to$ ST `badaa` (Note: ST orthography uses _b_, _d_, _g_ to represent voiceless unaspirated sounds [p, t, k]).
            
    - **GL**: **Retain voicing, or devoice to aspirated stops** (depending on dialect and position).
        
        - _Example_: PNEL `*badaː` $\to$ GL `pata` ([pʰata], some dialects retain voiced stops).
            
2. **PNEL Voiceless Unaspirated (`*p, *t, *k`)**:
    
    - **ST**: **Aspirated**.
        
        - _Example_: PNEL `*keg` (eat) $\to$ ST `keg` (ST orthography _p, t, k_ represent aspirated [pʰ, tʰ, kʰ]).
            
    - **GL**: **Retain unaspirated**.
        
        - _Example_: PNEL `*keg` $\to$ GL `keg` ([kek]).
            
3. **PNEL Uvulars (`*q, *χ`)**:
    
    - **ST**: Completely lost, merging forward into velars or dropping.
        
    - **GL**: `*q` merges into `k/g`; `*χ` evolves into uvular trill `/ʀ/` (spelled **r**).
        
        - _Example_: PNEL `*xiːra` (I) $\to$ ST `ia` (x lost) vs GL `hira` (x$\to$h).

### 1.3 Prosody and Stress

This is the fundamental cause of ST's grammatical analyticization.

- **PNEL Stress**: **Penultimate syllable**.
    
    - **GL**: Inherited this rule. Therefore, GL retains a complete system of suffixal endings, developing into an agglutinative language.
        
- **ST Evolution**: **Stress shift left to the initial syllable**.
    
    - **Consequence**: Final syllables weakened and dropped (Apocope) due to loss of stress.
        
    - _Example_: PNEL `*xíːra` (I) $\to$ ST `ía` (final syllable _ra_ dropped).
        
    - _Example_: PNEL `*kóme` (person) $\to$ ST `kóme` (fortuitously retained, but ending becomes light) vs GL `kóma`.

## 2 Morphology

Morphological divergence:

* **ST Evolution (Great Ending Extinction)**: Due to **stress shift left** to the initial syllable, case suffixes at word endings weakened and dropped. To salvage grammatical functions, ST underwent **"pronominal agglutination"** (birth of the definite article) and **"clitic prefixation"**.
* **GL Evolution (Bastion of Suffixes)**: By maintaining **penultimate stress**, word-final suffixes were protected. GL then "welded" these suffixes to roots via **vowel harmony** and further expanded the case system.

### 2.1 Nominal Morphology: Collapse and Reorganization of the Case System

We reconstruct PNEL as having **6 core cases**.

#### 2.1.1 Reconstruction of Proto-Cases

| **Case**  | **PNEL Reconstruction** | **ST (Truskean)** | **GL (Glömezher)** | **Evolution Analysis**                                           |
| ------- | ----------- | ------------ | ----------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| **Nominative**  | `*-Ø`       | `-ir` (definitization)  | `-Ø`        | Proto-language unmarked. ST's `-ir` may originate from phonetic changes and grammaticalization of a numeral.                    |
| **Accusative**  | `*-im`      | `-im`        | `-(h)ü/u`   | ST retains nasal coda. GL nasal vocalizes (`*m`$\to$`w`$\to$`u`) and is influenced by harmony. |
| **Genitive**  | `*-il`      | `-il`        | `-rö/xo`    | ST retains liquid `l`. GL undergoes liquid shift `l`$\to$`r` (Rhotacism).     |
| **Dative**  | `*-ma`      | `-ire` (innovation)  | `-m`        | GL retains the original dative `*-ma`. ST lost the original suffix, reconstructing it by borrowing a locative word.                |
| **Ablative**  | `*-ra`      | (lost)         | `-re/ra`    | GL retains. ST replaces it with preposition `we` (from).                          |
| **Locative** | `*-ta`      | `-irt`       | `-met/mat`  | ST `-ir-t` retains `t`. GL compounds `*ma-ta` (DAT+LOC)?          |

#### 2.1.2 The Definite Article Shift in Truskean

Due to ST's leftward stress shift, original case suffixes (e.g., `kóme-il` person-GEN) became weak syllables, facing the risk of dropping (`kóme-il` $\to$ `kóm(e)`).

To distinguish subject from non-subject, ST grammaticalized a demonstrative pronoun (likely a weakened form of `*se` or `*xira`) into a **definite suffix `-ir`**.

- This `-ir` became a new "grammatical anchor," protecting the remaining case markers.
    
- **Evolution chain**: PNEL `*kuti-ta` (stone-LOC) $\to$ Early ST `*kút(i)-ir-ta` (in that stone) $\to$ Late ST `cwek-irt`.

### 2.2 Verbal Morphology: The Contest Between Prefixes and Suffixes

PNEL's verb structure followed **SOV** logic, with function words (negation, modality) preceding the verb and aspect/tense markers following it.

PNEL verb template:
$$
[Proclitics] + [Root] + [Aspect/Tense Suffixes]
$$

#### 2.2.1 Evolution of Negation

- **PNEL**: `*mak` (or `*ma`), placed before the verb.
    
- **GL**: `mak-`. Although GL is a suffixing language, the negative is an extremely ancient retained **prefix**.
    
- **ST**: `mage` (independent adverb) / `a-` (prefix). ST's `a-` is a weakened form of `*mak` (`*mak` $\to$ `*ma` $\to$ `a`).
    

#### 2.2.2 Voice and Causative: The Rise of Prefixes (ST)

Why does ST have so many prefixes (`kwa-` passive, `de-` causative)?

- **Hypothesis**: PNEL likely expressed voice through syntactic means (e.g., auxiliary verbs or prepositions) at the beginning of the clause.
    
- **ST's Strategy**: With the establishment of SVO word order and erosion of endings, ST tended to fuse elements to the **left** of the verb (Aux/Prep) into the verb.
    
    - _Example_: PNEL `*de` (do/make) + `Verb` $\to$ ST `de-Verb` (causative prefix).
        
- **GL's Strategy**: GL tends to postpose and agglutinate function words.
    
    - _Example_: `Verb` + `*da` (do) $\to$ GL `Verb-ddad` (causative suffix).
        
    - _Note_: This is why ST's causative is the prefix `de-`, while GL's is the suffix `-det/-ddad`. They originate from the same proto-root `*de/*da` (do), but fused at different positions.
        

#### 2.2.3 Tense-Aspect System: Different Grammaticalization Paths

The proto-language likely had only a simple "imperfective/perfective" contrast; complex tense-aspect developed later.

1. **Past Tense**:
    
    - **PNEL**: `*-gu` (go/over).
        
    - **ST**: Corrupted into an independent auxiliary particle `gu`, placed after the verb (`keg gu` eat PAST).
        
    - **GL**: Grammaticalized into the suffix `-(h)uj`.

## 3 Syntax and Pronoun Reconstruction

This chapter aims to recover the underlying syntactic logic of PNEL. Based on in-depth comparison of Great Camp Glömezher (conservative branch) and Strait Truskean (innovative branch), the Academy confirms that PNEL's native word order was SOV (Subject-Object-Verb) and strictly followed **head-final** principles (i.e., modifiers precede the head).

Truskean's unique **SVO + head-initial** structure, and its distinctive **"clause-final particle"** mechanism, are not random evolutions. They are the product of the ancestors' reconstruction of the original grammar using a "topic-comment" strategy during the pidgin stage of the early imperial trade lingua franca.

### 3.1 Pronoun System: Retention and Divergence

The pronoun system shows extremely high stability, providing the most solid evidence for the genetic relationship. We note that the two languages retain almost identical roots but diverged in plural marking.
