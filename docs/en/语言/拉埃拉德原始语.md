# Proto-Laelad

!!! wiki "Entry Information"
    | Attribute | Description |
    | :--- | :--- |
    | **Self-designation** | tsʰer |
    | **Language Family** | Northeastern Laelad Language Family |
    | **Morphological Type** | Agglutinative |
    | **Basic Word Order** | SVO (Subject-Verb-Object) |
    | **Primary Distribution** | Laaerad Region (Cataclysm Period) |
    | **Writing System** | Proto-Laelad Script (Roman Orthography used in this article) |

<b><ruby>Proto-Laelad<rt>Proto-Laelad</rt></ruby></b> (self-designation <b><ruby>tsʰer<rt>tsʰer</rt></ruby></b>) is the earliest known language of the Laaerad region, identified as the common ancestor of the Northeastern Laelad languages. Records of this language primarily originate from the only discovered Cataclysm period ruins found to date.

This language exhibits the following notable features:

- **Highly Agglutinative**: Expresses complex grammatical functions through a rich system of prefixes and suffixes.
- **Flexible Word Order**: Basic word order is Subject-Verb-Object (SVO), but can be flexibly adjusted based on emphasis.
- **Strict Morphology**: Nouns, verbs, and adjectives all possess rigorous rules for case, tense, aspect, and derivation.

### 1.2 Phonological Rules

- **Syllable Structure**: Relatively strict, allowing a maximum of two consonant clusters.
- **Geminate Simplification**: When consecutive identical consonants occur, they are simplified to a single consonant.
- **Assimilation Rules**:
    - Consonant `m` dissimilates to `n` before `t`, `d`, `n`.
    - Consonant `n` assimilates to `m` before `p`, `b`, `m`.

## 2 Morphology: Nouns

The Proto-Laelad noun system exhibits significant inflectional characteristics, possessing a complete case system and strong derivational capabilities.

### 2.1 Case Declension

| **Case** | **Suffix** | **Example** | **Meaning** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Nominative** | Ø (none) | **kome** | person (subject) |
| **Accusative** | -ū / -hū | **komehū** | person (object) |
| **Genitive** | -la | **komela** | of the person |
| **Dative** | -um / -m | **komeum** | to/for the person |
| **Instrumental** | -śe | **komeśe** | with/by the person |
| **Locative** | -met | **komemet** | at/with/towards the person |

### 2.2 Other Morphology and Derivation

- **Plural Marker**: Suffix <b><ruby>-ti<rt>plural</rt></ruby></b>
    - Example: `kome` (person) → `kometi` (people)
- **Negative Prefix**: <b><ruby>a-<rt>negative</rt></ruby></b> (variants `ah-`/`aʔ-`)
    - Example: `akome` (no person/non-person)
- **Part-of-Speech Conversion**:
    - **Nominalization of Nouns**: Suffix **-kem**. Example: `leuk` (fire) → `leukkem` (to burn)
    - **Adjectivalization of Nouns**: Suffix **-tʰom**.

## 3 Morphology: Verbs

Verbs typically function as predicates in sentences, possessing a complex system of aspect, tense, and mood suffixes.

### 3.1 Morphological Structure

The morphological construction of verbs follows a fixed order: **Root + Aspect Suffix + Tense Suffix**.

#### 3.1.1 Aspect

| **Aspect** | **Suffix** | **Explanation** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Simple Aspect** | Ø | Verb base form |
| **Progressive Aspect** | **-kar** | Action in progress |
| **Perfective Aspect** | **-po** | Action completed |
| **Progressive Perfective Aspect** | **-karpo** | Emphasizes an action that continued for a period and has ended |

#### 3.1.2 Tense

| **Tense** | **Suffix** | **Explanation** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Present Tense** | Ø | Verb base form |
| **Past Tense** | **-gum** | Action that occurred in the past |
| **Future Tense** | **-pat** | Action that will occur |
| **Past Future Tense** | **-gumpat** | Action that was going to occur from a past perspective |
| **Subjunctive Mood** | **-es** | Hypothetical situation (similar to English subjunctive "were") |

### 3.2 Functional Suffixes and Derivation

#### 3.2.1 Part-of-Speech Conversion

- **Nominalization of Verbs**: Suffix **-dra**
- **Adjectivalization of Verbs**: Suffix **-lap**

#### 3.2.2 Imperative Mood

- **Plain Imperative**: Suffix **-pas**
- **Strong/Polite Imperative**: Suffix **-pasingo** (expresses a stronger or more formal request)
    - Example: `Lasotmorgū kʰegpas!` (Eat the noodles!)
    - Example: `Hirahū āntshe shniompasingo.` (Please look at me properly.)

### 3.3 Prefix System (Voice and Polarity)

Prefixes are primarily used to change the voice and polarity of verbs.

| **Prefix** | **Name** | **Function** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **eb-** | Passive | Forms passive sentences |
| **ded-** | Causative | Forms causative sentences |
| **mak-** | Negative | Forms negative statements or questions |

#### 3.3.1 Syntactic Structures

- **Passive Sentence**: `NounA(Nom) + eb-Verb + NounB(Ins)`
    - Meaning: NounB performed an action on NounA.
- **Causative Sentence**: `NounA(Nom) + ded-Verb + NounB(Ins/Acc)`
    - Meaning: NounA caused NounB to perform an action.

#### 3.3.2 Prefix Concatenation Rules

1.  **Negative + Passive**: The negative prefix `mak-` must precede the passive prefix `eb-`.
2.  **Negative + Causative**: Order determines meaning.
    - `mak-` + `ded-`: **not cause, not force** (negates the causative act).
    - `ded-` + `mak-`: **not allow, forbid** (cause to not be able/do).

## 4 Morphology: Adjectives

Adjectives generally function as attributes and are **postposed** to the noun they modify.

### 4.1 Comparison

- **Comparative**: Suffix **-pʰa**
- **Superlative**: Suffix **-pʰon**

### 4.2 Derivation

- **Nominalization of Adjectives**:
    - **Concrete Noun**: Suffix **-ot**
    - **Abstract Noun**: Suffix **-kʰar**
- **Verbalization of Adjectives**: Suffix **-unsi**
    - Example: `drodsāpunsi` (to feel happy)
    - With causative: `dedsāpunsi` (to raise/lift up)
- **Adverbialization of Adjectives**: Suffix **-tśe**

!!! info "Etymological Note"
    The adverbial suffix **-tśe** originates from the adjectival abstract nominalization suffix (-kʰar) followed by the instrumental case (-śe), i.e., *-kʰarśe*. Through sound change and contraction to *-kśe*, which violated phonological rules (double plosive/affricate cluster), it eventually evolved into **-tśe**.

### 4.3 Inflection Rules

After nominalization or verbalization, adjectives fully follow the declension or conjugation rules of nouns or verbs.
> Example: `dedmakdroddūmunsies drodum` (if one were to spare a heart from sorrow)

## 6 Syntax and Example Sentences

### 6.1 Basic Sentence Patterns

Although Proto-Laelad has flexible word order, it fundamentally follows the **Subject - Verb - Object** (SVO) structure, with modifiers (like adjectives) postposed.

#### Tense and Aspect Examples

| **English Meaning** | **Proto-Laelad** | **Grammatical Analysis** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| The sun shines. | **Hēm ileunsi.** | sun shine-VERBALIZER |
| The sun is shining. | **Hēm ileunsikar.** | sun shine-VERB-PROG |
| The sun will shine. | **Hēm ileunsipat.** | sun shine-VERB-FUT |
| The sun has been shining. | **Hēm ileunsikarpogum.** | sun shine-VERB-PROG-PERF-PAST |
| The sun shines brightly. | **Hēm ileunsi iletśe.** | sun shine-VERB bright-ADVERB |
| The bright sun shines. | **Hēm ile ileunsi.** | sun bright shine-VERB |

#### Common Phrases

- **Jepompas dorāotmet!** 😠
    - (Go away!)
    - walk-IMP far-NOMINALIZER-LOC!

- **Śoro jepompasingo.**
    - (You should go.)
    - you walk-STRONG.IMP.

- **ān! Ma nāmohu shorola keem?**
    - (Hello! What is your name?)
    - good! what name-ACC you-GEN copula?

### 6.2 Text Translation Excerpts

#### 6.2.1 Excerpt from *Shijing* (Classic of Poetry), "Xiao Ya - Cai Wei"

> **Original**: 昔我往矣，杨柳依依。今我来思，雨雪霏霏。行道迟迟，载渴载饥。我心伤悲，莫知我哀。

**Laelad Translation:**

> Jepomgum hira, wise ipa setshekargum.
> (walk-PAST I, tree green stand-PROG-PAST)
>
> Lepkar hira, pamorkem rigurkem.
> (come-PROG I, water-fall-VERB snow-VERB)
>
> Lōsmet las akʰedomkem a apakdomkem.
> (road-LOC long hunger-VERB and thirst-VERB)
>
> Drod hirala droddūmusikar, akome droddūmkʰarū hirala tēs.
> (heart I-GEN heart-die-VERB-PROG, no-person heart-die-NOMINALIZER-ACC I-GEN know)
